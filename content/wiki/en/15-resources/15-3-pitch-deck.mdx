## Typical slide content (aligned with formal deck)

The following modules are the reference order after combining industry practice and WCN narrative; the actual number of pages can be combined, but it is not recommended to disrupt the logic.

<Steps>
  <Step title="cover and sentence" description="Project name, stage labels, contact information; avoid excessive slogans." />
  <Step title="Pain points and opportunities" description="Structural failures in the connection between capital and projects are supported by observable phenomena rather than pure adjectives." />
  <Step title="What is WCN" description="The differences between network boundaries, participants, and platform or single chain ecology - one picture is better than multiple paragraphs of text." />
  <Step title="Product and user paths" description="Minimum closed-loop diagram of Deal/Proof/node collaboration; short interface or flow chart optional." />
  <Step title="Architecture summary" description="A one-page overview of the layered architecture; details point to the white paper, avoiding the need to expand to the underlying protocols on the Deck." />
  <Step title="PoB and trust mechanism" description="Why business proof is important, how to review it, main abuse scenarios and mitigation ideas (honestly write down incomplete solutions)." />
  <Step title="Agent (if already landed)" description="Automation scope, permissions and manual review boundaries; relationship with PoB and logs." />
  <Step title="Business model and stage assumptions" description="Revenue sources, unit economic logic, and sensitive parameters at the current stage are expressed in intervals or qualitatively." />
  <Step title="Traction and Milestones" description="Verifiable outputs consistent with roadmap stages; use conditionals for future milestones (if × is reached, then enter ×)." />
  <Step title="Competition and ecological niche" description="Comparison matrix with FA, exchange Launchpad, general DAO, etc. to avoid disparaging specific opponents." />
  <Step title="Team and Consultants" description="Core members and relevant experience; advisory roles and true participation levels are truthfully disclosed." />
  <Step title="Financing/cooperation requirements" description="This round or cooperation demands, fund use or resource type, and time window." />
  <Step title="Risk and Legal Summary" description="Key risk points; it is clear that this Deck does not constitute investment advice; see the legal statement and white paper for details." />
</Steps>

## Division of labor with white papers

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Deck responsible" icon="mic">Emotion curves, key points, live Q&A hooks; charts and analogies help non-expert audiences build mental models.</Card>
  <Card title="White Paper Responsible" icon="book-open">Definitions, assumptions, boundary conditions and cited document-level details; for due diligence and legal verification step by step.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="info">A good deck can still be forwarded individually after the speech: the title of each page should be its own unit of information; avoid fragmented pictures that "can only be understood by the speaker reading the manuscript".</Callout>

---

## quality red line

<Statement>Deck should neither obscure regulatory and securities law sensitive statements for the sake of "looking good" nor should unaudited forecast charts replace staged facts; exaggerated promises will damage consistency with white papers, FAQs and legal statements, and backfire on trust during the due diligence phase.</Statement>
