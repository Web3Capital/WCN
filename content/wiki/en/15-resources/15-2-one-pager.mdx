# One-Pager pursues scannability, not argument completeness.

<MetaGrid>
  <Meta label="core positioning" value="A very short summary for first touch, with clear next steps" />
  <Meta label="Typical scenario" value="Cold email attachments, exhibition business card follow-up, community profile pinned to the top, IR packet cover page" />
  <Meta label="Downstream materials" value="White paper (in-depth reading), Pitch Deck (conference), official website or GitBook (continuous updates)" />
</MetaGrid>

## What is usually included on a single page

The professional One-Pager is organized according to fixed information blocks to facilitate horizontal comparison of other projects:

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Positioning in one sentence" icon="pin">What network WCN is, who it serves, and the differences from "traditional FA/single platform" are written in testable terms.</Card>
  <Card title="Issues and timing" icon="timer">Why existing structures fail with information, execution, or trust; why now is the window for networked collaboration.</Card>
  <Card title="Solution and product form" icon="puzzle">Node, Deal, Proof, PoB, Agent, etc. should be placed on one line each at a level understandable by non-technical readers to avoid overlapping terms.</Card>
  <Card title="Stages and Progress Signals" icon="trending-up">Current stage (such as Network MVP), quantitative or qualitative signals that can be disclosed; unverified items are honestly marked as "in progress".</Card>
  <Card title="Cooperation/participation portal" icon="door-open">Corresponding contact paths or forms for investors, strategic parties, and node candidates to avoid a single general email address.</Card>
  <Card title="Compliance reminder" icon="scale">The bottom briefly points to the full disclaimer or "See the legal section of the white paper for details" and does not take up the length of the main text.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Suggestions for using the process

<Steps>
  <Step title="before sending" description="Confirm that the numbers and stage descriptions on the single page are consistent with the current version of the white paper and deck; check that the logo, domain name, and contact email are official channels." />
  <Step title="When sending" description="Use 3 to 5 sentences to summarize a single page in the email body; the attachment naming convention (such as WCN_OnePager_YYYYMM) facilitates archiving by the other party." />
  <Step title="follow up" description="If the other party opens up the depth, proactively provide a link to the white paper and a 30-minute Deck meeting; avoid repeated promotions before reading the long article." />
</Steps>

<Callout type="info">One-Pager is not a "reduced version of the white paper": Reduction will cause the font size to be too small or delete all borders; the correct approach is to delete the arguments and retain the conclusions and quotations.</Callout>

## limitations and division of labor

<Statement>One-Pager does not carry PoB details, Token/NFT legal characterization, or compliance conclusions in specific jurisdictions - these fall within the scope of the white paper text and the work of legal advisors.</Statement>
