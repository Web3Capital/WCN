## White Paper Responsibilities

The white paper undertakes three types of functions: **complete argument** (hypothesis, mechanism and counterexample), **auditable citation** (cross-reference of definition, stage and disclaimer), **internal and external alignment** (reduce caliber drift caused by verbal explanation). It does not replace the rhythm of the road show, nor does it replace the terms of the contract; contracts and regulatory filings are subject to formal legal documents.

## Typical chapter structure (for comparison with PDF)

The official white paper PDF should be organized into the following modules according to readability and citation requirements (specific titles can be fine-tuned, but the logical order is recommended to be maintained):

<Steps>
  <Step title="Abstracts and Reader's Guide" description="One sentence positioning, scope of this document, misunderstandings that are not applicable (for example, it is not equivalent to a securities prospectus)." />
  <Step title="Background and problem statement" description="Information, trust and execution friction in the connection between capital and projects; aspects that can be improved by the Web3 native network." />
  <Step title="WCN Definition and Participants" description="Network boundaries, responsibilities of each role, and differences from a single public chain or a single DAO." />
  <Step title="Architecture and Process" description="The smallest closed loop of Deal Room, Proof Desk, and node collaboration; the connection point with subsequent settlement/asset stages." />
  <Step title="Mechanical depth" description="PoB rules outline, functional description of Node NFT or identity carrier (separated from legal qualifications), and Agent design principles." />
  <Step title="Economic Model and Stage Assumptions" description="Portal for cost, incentive and stage sensitivity analysis; aligned with roadmap milestones." />
  <Step title="Risks, Compliance and Disclaimers" description="Consistent with or cross-referenced to 15.5 Legal Statements; Standard Paragraphs Governing and Forward-Looking Statements." />
</Steps>

## Who should read the white paper first?

- **Financial Investors**: Used to check whether narratives and numerical assumptions are consistent before term sheets or in-depth meetings.  
- **Strategy and Partners**: Evaluate interface responsibilities, data sharing and co-branding boundaries.  
- **Node Candidate**: Understand PoB, obligations and network phases before committing time and resources.  
- **Internal Products and Markets**: As the upstream of the "single source of truth" for external discourse and customer-facing materials.

<Callout type="info">If the other party is extremely short on time, the One-Pager or Deck fragment should be sent first, and it should be clearly stated that "the complete technology and mechanism can be found in Chapter × of the white paper" - to avoid short materials being misinterpreted as complete disclosure.</Callout>

## What a white paper shouldn’t do

<Statement>White papers are not suitable as "sole sales tools" or "substitutes for legal opinions": the former is completed by One-Pager, Deck and meetings; the latter must be issued by a licensed legal advisor in a specific jurisdiction.</Statement>

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Non-real-time status screen" icon="chart-bar">On-chain data, specific Deal lists, and real-time KPIs should be displayed on product or data dashboards, and the white paper retains the methodology and representative examples.</Card>
  <Card title="Non-Personalized Term Sheet" icon="scroll">The signed text shall prevail for the node agreement, investment agreement, confidentiality obligations, etc.</Card>
</CardGrid>
