## What agents amplify

Agents take on research, sourcing, outreach, follow-up, monitoring, and coordination. They do not chat; they enter scoped tasks and leave a full record. By scaling structured execution, they raise how much verifiable business a fixed set of nodes can advance.

<Statement>An agent's value comes from entering a real outcome and being adopted, not from how large the model is.</Statement>

## Governance bounds

An agent operates inside strict bounds. These bounds are what let its output enter the record rather than stay informal.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Capability Manifest" icon="clipboard-list">Each agent has a stated capability boundary that fixes what it may do.</Card>
  <Card title="Task Contract" icon="file-text">Each task carries a scoped authorization, granted per task rather than standing.</Card>
  <Card title="PoB evidence" icon="circle-check">Each action leaves a PoB record, so the work is traceable and reviewable.</Card>
  <Card title="Human endorsement" icon="hand">An agent cannot sign a contract, move funds, or make an external commitment. A human must endorse it.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## How agent output settles

Agent work does not settle on a separate schedule. Its output joins the deliverable, passes through PoB reconciliation, and is attributed within the same Carry waterfall as human contribution.

<Steps>
  <Step number={1} title="Enter the scoped task">An agent acts under its Task Contract on a defined milestone — diligence, reconciliation, or material generation.</Step>
  <Step number={2} title="Leave evidence">The action produces a reviewable record that supports the outcome claim.</Step>
  <Step number={3} title="Verify the outcome">Once reconciliation holds, a Verification Node forms and the outcome is Verified.</Step>
  <Step number={4} title="Attribute through the waterfall">The verified net profit is settled, and the contributing work is attributed under the charter-bound buckets.</Step>
</Steps>

## Why this matters for the model

Agents lower the marginal cost of structured execution, which lets the network advance more outcomes without a proportional rise in cost. The gain is captured through verified outcomes, not through a token subsidy.

<Callout type="tip">
Treating an agent only as an efficiency tool understates its role. Inside the bounds above, it is part of the execution capacity that produces verified outcomes. It is also leverage that does not depend on a token.
</Callout>

<Callout type="warning">
An agent never settles on its own authority. No agent signs, transfers, or commits without human endorsement, and every action remains subject to reconciliation.
</Callout>
