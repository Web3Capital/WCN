## Why you need a transition layer: Failure modes on both ends

**Never transition**: After expansion, there will still be a single point of decision-making, which will lead to bottlenecks, insufficient regional knowledge, and overloaded responsibilities; compliance incidents caused by the **disconnection between the headquarters and the front line** of some TradFi institutions during cross-border expansion.

**Excessive transition**: Opening key voting when PoB and auditing have not yet supported "evidence-based governance" will repeat Compound/Beanstalk-type **financialization of governance rights and lightning manipulation** risks.

Transitional governance is an intermediate state driven by verifiable milestones, rather than the slogan "DAOization".

## Typical signals that trigger transitions (illustrative, non-exhaustive)

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Node and area density" icon="users">Active nodes in multiple regions, repeatable collaboration and dispute samples, support a permanent coordination mechanism rather than a temporary group.</Card>
  <Card title="PoB and settlement volume" icon="chart-bar">The certification and settlement path is stable, and audit SLA, objection escalation and review rules can be defined.</Card>
  <Card title="role specialization" icon="building-2">The boundaries between legal affairs, compliance, security, finance and operations are clear, and a functional committee can be formed to undertake authorization.</Card>
  <Card title="Internal control and traces" icon="clipboard-list">Key actions can be audited and replayed, meeting "reasonable procedures" and investigation cooperation expectations in multiple jurisdictions.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Can be delegated gradually vs. not easily delegated

Drawing on the constitutional boundaries of **ENS** and the parameter governance experience of **Uniswap**: **expanded participation** should fall in areas with "procedures, evidence, and revocable authorization"; **high-risk areas** maintain the exclusive responsibility of the exec/board.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Can be structured and delegated (example)" icon="circle-check">Deal process suggestions for collaboration with nodes, regional market feedback aggregation, non-final review opinions, educational and ecological initiatives, and parameter suggestions within boundaries (subject to approval by the responsible layer and time lock arrangements if applicable).</Card>
  <Card title="Not easy to delegate" icon="lock">Entity and licensing strategy, regulatory communication and enforcement response, KYC/AML and sanctions screening policy, major litigation and arbitration strategy, core system and security incident command, brand and major external commitments, final signing authority for key settlement and fund custody.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="warning">The success indicators of transitional governance are **reduced decision-making delays, increased implementation consistency, and complete audit trails**; if only "more proposals increase", it is usually a sign of failure.</Callout>

## Analogy to Traditional Tiered Licensing

**Public Company Committee**: Audit, remuneration, strategy and other sub-committees are accountable to the board of directors - WCN can be analogized to **board of directors/committee + clear authorization document**, rather than a referendum for day-to-day operations.

**Revision of Exchange Rules**: Regular consultation period, committee review and regulatory filing - corresponding to WCN's **node/industry input → structured review → approval by the responsible layer**.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Define Authorization Charter">State the committee’s authority, terms of office, conflict disclosure and escalation paths.</Step>
  <Step title="Aligned with 11.4">Permanent restricted areas are not accessible to any "transitional pilot".</Step>
  <Step title="Embed evidence standards">Linked to PoB and internal control logs to avoid "opinion politics" replacing facts.</Step>
  <Step title="Regular review">Review whether authorization has been abused or expired on a quarterly or major event basis.</Step>
</Steps>

<Statement>Transitional governance is the bridge between centralization and decentralization: without it, the network is either dead or ill-prepared and repeats the governance tuition Web3 has paid.</Statement>
