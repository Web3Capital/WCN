## The eight-layer command stack

WCN does not start from one-person-one-vote. Authority runs through eight layers. Each layer holds a function, and each binds authority to responsibility on the same chain.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="L0 · Constitution" icon="scroll">The highest constraint. It defines the non-votable items and the bounds on waterfall parameters. Every layer below operates inside these limits.</Card>
  <Card title="L1 · Foundation Board" icon="landmark">Fiduciary duty and oversight. The board holds the founder accountable under the thresholds defined in 11.3.</Card>
  <Card title="L2 · Council of Founders" icon="users">Governance across the 100 founding seats. The Council can open a strategic review and votes on the highest-bar decisions.</Card>
  <Card title="L3 · Conductor (Stephen)" icon="user">The commander. The Conductor sets strategy and holds final coordination during the founding period.</Card>
  <Card title="L4 · GP / execution team" icon="briefcase">Daily execution and external delivery. The team turns strategy into deals, contracts, and outcomes.</Card>
  <Card title="L5 · Agent execution layer" icon="bot">Structured task execution. Each agent works inside a task contract and retains a PoB record of its actions.</Card>
  <Card title="L6 · Country / City / Track nodes" icon="globe">Regional and track coordination. These nodes route local deal flow and represent their market or sector.</Card>
  <Card title="L7 · Service / Standard nodes" icon="handshake">Professional service and basic participation. These layers deliver scoped work without a governance seat.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Why the order runs top-down

The Constitution sits above every actor, including the founder. It fixes the limits no vote can cross and the bounds on how value is distributed. Strategy then flows from the Conductor through the execution team, the agent layer, and the node tiers.

<Callout type="info">Authority descends through the stack; responsibility ascends back up it. A node or agent executes a scoped task, leaves a PoB record, and remains accountable to the layer that authorized the work.</Callout>

## Why founder-led during the founding period

WCN names its posture without hedging. The network is founder-led while node density, PoB credibility, and regional compliance infrastructure are still forming. Founder-led direction serves three goals at this stage.

<Steps>
  <Step title="One direction, one external voice">Major external statements come from a named responsible layer. This reduces conflicting commitments to the market, partners, and regulators.</Step>
  <Step title="One priority order">Capital and people are limited during cold start. A single priority order prevents the delivery paralysis that multi-center bargaining produces.</Step>
  <Step title="One correction path">Security incidents, legal correspondence, and regulatory inquiries need a response in hours or days. A clear command path makes that speed possible.</Step>
  <Step title="A path to delegate later">As nodes, PoB, and dispute handling mature, defined matters move down the stack under the rules in 11.2. The structure reserves the interfaces for that shift.</Step>
</Steps>

## What founder-led does not mean

Founder-led is a structural choice, not unchecked power. The Constitution binds the founder. The Foundation Board and the Council of Founders hold two accountability thresholds, set out in 11.3. Six items can never be put to a vote, set out in 11.4.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Bound by the Constitution" icon="scroll">L0 defines the non-votable items and the waterfall-parameter bounds. The founder cannot cross them.</Card>
  <Card title="Answerable to two layers" icon="scale">The Foundation Board and the Council of Founders can remove the founder for cause, or force a strategic review for missed milestones.</Card>
  <Card title="Recorded, not described" icon="clipboard-list">Agent actions and settled outcomes leave PoB records. Decisions are traceable to a named owner, not to a vague collective.</Card>
  <Card title="Designed to delegate" icon="shuffle">The stack is built so authority can move down it as the network matures, without releasing the non-votable list.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Statement>Founder-led governance is a stage strategy, not an ideology. Its legitimacy rests on fiduciary oversight, the Constitution, and a network still in formation. See 11.3 for accountability and 11.4 for the permanent limits.</Statement>
