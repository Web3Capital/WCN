## What WCN can claim today

These statements describe the network as it operates now. Each rests on a delivered fact, not a projection.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Node access and seats" icon="circle-check">WCN expands the node network under written rules. Rights and obligations follow the charter and system permissions. This is not a fully autonomous onchain DAO.</Card>
  <Card title="Deal and resource coordination" icon="circle-check">Projects, capital, and services collaborate in a structured workflow. Outcomes depend on the participants and their diligence. WCN does not guarantee any deal or valuation.</Card>
  <Card title="PoB verification" icon="circle-check">Evidence submission, review, and attribution operate and continue to iterate. PoB is an internal verification mechanism, not automatic third-party legal or accounting certification.</Card>
  <Card title="Seat, service, and settlement fees" icon="circle-check">Fees are collected inside a contract and tax framework. Income fluctuates with the market and is not a stable cash-flow commitment.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## What WCN does not claim

These statements mark the limits. WCN does not imply or promise any of them at this stage.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Full decentralization" icon="ban">WCN does not claim a fully decentralized governance. The network is founder-led, moving through the transition in 11.2, with the non-votable items in 11.4 fixed.</Card>
  <Card title="License coverage" icon="ban">WCN does not imply it holds specific licenses across all operating jurisdictions. The multi-jurisdiction compliance structure is a Phase 3 target, not a present fact.</Card>
  <Card title="Returns or token value" icon="ban">WCN does not represent fixed returns, capital protection, or regulatory endorsement for node rights, any token, or any record. Sustainability does not depend on issuing a token.</Card>
  <Card title="Onchain as a guarantee" icon="ban">WCN does not treat "onchain" as sufficient for compliance or success. Placing a vote onchain provides neither security nor compliance on its own.</Card>
  <Card title="Unconditional access" icon="ban">WCN does not open membership without screening. Node quality and compliance checks remain central to access, and open borders track risk-control capacity.</Card>
  <Card title="Agents replacing sign-off" icon="ban">Agents assist with decisions and documentation. They do not replace the sign-off of legal counsel, accountants, or compliance officers, and tool overreach remains a risk.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Known limitations

WCN states its current limits plainly, so nodes and investors can weigh them.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Governance" icon="construction">The committee charter, cross-region representation, and dispute adjudication are still being built. Emergency response relies on the core team, not distributed voting.</Card>
  <Card title="Compliance" icon="scale">Under parallel jurisdiction requirements, the default conservative posture can delay or withhold functions in some regions.</Card>
  <Card title="Technical" icon="shield">Any deployed smart contract requires independent audit and a vulnerability-response budget. Off-chain systems carry security and availability risk.</Card>
  <Card title="Market" icon="chart-bar">The crypto capital cycle affects deal density and fee income. This sits partly outside the quality of the governance design.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Why state the limits in public

Clear boundaries do not weaken the network. They reduce the gap between expectation and delivery that creates legal and reputational risk later.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Diligence rewards candor">Professional capital judges integrity by the capabilities a network does not claim. Hiding limits works against valuations and terms.</Step>
  <Step title="Consistent disclosure beats repair">Proactive, consistent disclosure is stronger than correction after the fact, in any dialogue with a regulator or partner.</Step>
  <Step title="Nodes commit on facts">Nodes invest resources with informed expectations. Accurate limits reduce the risk of collective action after a narrative oversells.</Step>
</Steps>

<Statement>Only a network willing to say "we are not there yet" turns roadmap milestones into verifiable deliverables. The alternative is to treat the future as a permanent excuse.</Statement>
