## The six never-votable items

The Constitution (L0) defines these six items. No node vote, token weight, or majority decision can override them. They remain with the responsible layers under the framework of law and charter.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="1 · Core privacy rights" icon="lock-keyhole">A node's core privacy rights cannot be violated by majority vote. A majority cannot expose or strip the privacy that membership protects.</Card>
  <Card title="2 · AML and compliance" icon="shield">AML and compliance enforcement cannot be bypassed. No vote can exempt the network from anti-money-laundering obligations or sanctions screening.</Card>
  <Card title="3 · Formed PoB records" icon="clipboard-list">A formed PoB record cannot be altered after the fact. Once an outcome is verified onto the proof ledger, no vote can rewrite it.</Card>
  <Card title="4 · Signed legal agreements" icon="scroll">A signed legal agreement cannot be unilaterally breached. A majority cannot vote to walk away from a binding commitment.</Card>
  <Card title="5 · Absent minority nodes" icon="users">An emergency motion that harms absent minority nodes cannot pass. Urgency cannot be used to strip the interests of nodes not in the room.</Card>
  <Card title="6 · Long-term sustainability" icon="trending-up">A short-term payout that damages long-term sustainability cannot pass. The network cannot vote itself a near-term gain at the cost of its future.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Why these items stay outside any vote

Each item protects something a majority could otherwise damage under pressure. Three forces make open voting unsafe in these domains.

<Callout type="warning">Short-term pressure can push a majority to override actions that are legally necessary but locally unwelcome. A fixed list removes that option before the pressure arrives, rather than during it.</Callout>

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Rights are not majority-disposable" icon="shield">Privacy, signed agreements, and the interests of absent nodes are commitments. A vote cannot dissolve a duty the network already owes.</Card>
  <Card title="Records are settled, not reopened" icon="clipboard-list">A formed PoB record is the network's memory of a verified outcome. Reopening it to a vote would make every record provisional.</Card>
  <Card title="Compliance is not optional" icon="scale">AML and sanctions obligations bind the responsible parties under law. A community vote cannot waive them without creating real liability.</Card>
  <Card title="The future has no vote" icon="trending-up">People in the room can favor a payout today. The list protects the long-term interest that absent and future participants depend on.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## How the list is enforced

A list is only as strong as its enforcement. WCN binds it in writing and at the technical layer, and lets it grow but never shrink without due process.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Written and disclosed">The non-votable items appear in the charter, the governance materials, and the node documents. They are not informal red lines.</Step>
  <Step title="Enforced in the system">Permissions and contracts are configured so a single proposal cannot reach a non-votable item. The block is structural, not procedural.</Step>
  <Step title="Can grow, not shrink">As compliance requirements change, the list can expand. It contracts only through a formal charter revision and the proper legal process.</Step>
</Steps>

<Statement>Knowing what cannot be voted on protects the network from legitimizing harm under pressure. The six items guard privacy, compliance, settled records, signed commitments, absent minorities, and the long term.</Statement>
