## What is the national master node?

The national master node is the primary organizational seat of WCN in a single sovereign jurisdiction. It is responsible for connecting the national node subgraph (cities, industries, functions) to the global network, and reducing order collisions, repeated due diligence and relationship overdrafts within the country.

Typical scenarios include: coordinating the RWA plan quotations of three capital nodes for the same listed company; designating the preferred legal consultant and audit path for overseas project parties entering the country; unifying the external schedule and media caliber during the large-scale conference cycle.

<Statement>What the national master node buys out is not the market, but its accountable role in "the quality and order of the country's network."</Statement>

## Boundaries and anti-patterns

  <Card title="Non-unlimited agents" description="You cannot implicitly sign obligations beyond the authorization matrix on behalf of WCN or partners; analogously to an investment bank MD, you still need legal and compliance approval." icon="ban" />

  <Card title="non-title rental" description="Value should come from organizational closed loops and PoB verifiable outputs, rather than the social premium of the seat itself." icon="ban" />
  <Card title="Governed and audited" description="Permission classification, log traces, conflict of interest declaration and review procedures are bound to national nodes." icon="ban" />

<Callout type="info">
In some markets, national master nodes may coexist with licensed entities: the WCN side provides protocols and routing, and local licensed parties provide compliance shells. The product design needs to separate the "brand" and "legally responsible entity" to avoid the node title being misinterpreted as regulatory endorsement.
</Callout>
