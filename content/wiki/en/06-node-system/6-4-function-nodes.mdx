## Overview of function nodes

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Project node" description="For example, L2, RWA platform, payment application: carry roadmap, compliance strategy, financing rhythm and currency listing requirements; they are the source of demand and asset narrative." icon="package" />
  <Card title="capital node" description="For example, a single company's allocation range of US$5 million to US$50 million, or a seed-focused crypto fund: determine the valuation logic, term sheet, and co-investment rhythm." icon="coins" />
  <Card title="service node" description="For example, Top-tier law firm token group, smart contract auditor, brand public relations retainer: turn verbal recommendations into signature pages and deliverables." icon="wrench" />
  <Card title="distribution node" description="For example, Tier1 CEX currency listing office, leading KOL matrix, large-scale summit IP: undertaking TGE, market making packages and communication schedules." icon="radio" />
  <Card title="Regional node" description="National master node and city node: organize activities, government relations and local capital in the geographical dimension; see 6.5 and 6.6 for details." icon="globe" />
  <Card title="Agent node" description="For example, 7×24 monitoring of financing rounds and automatic drafting of first-round contact emails: model boundaries, manual review points, and log retention strategies must be disclosed." icon="bot" />
</CardGrid>

<Statement>Divide functions first, and then divide responsibilities and contribution measurement; otherwise the roles of the same organization in different links will be mixed, and the PoB will not be able to separate the introduction fee, execution fee and success fee.</Statement>

## Comparison of functional division of labor and TradFi tabletop

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Project node" description="Corresponding to the corporate account department: Define transaction motivation and timetable." icon="target" />
  <Card title="capital node" description="Corresponding buyer sales to capital markets: matching products and constraints." icon="coins" />
  <Card title="service node" description="Corresponding to the middle office and third-party consultants: put the terms into documents and code." icon="wrench" />
  <Card title="distribution node" description="Corresponding to ECM / sales transaction interface: turning stories into order flow and liquidity." icon="radio" />
  <Card title="Regional node" description="Corresponding branch coverage: localized relationships and regulatory communications." icon="globe" />
  <Card title="Agent node" description="Corresponding quantification and middle-office scripts: Amplify standard processes, but do not replace the disclosure point of regulated judgment." icon="bot" />
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="tip">
Regional nodes appear side by side with functions in the 6.3 overview, but are often implemented as superimposed labels of "function + geography"; in 6.4, it is emphasized that their functional semantics are "local organization and implementation" to avoid confusion with pure online capital or service nodes.
</Callout>
