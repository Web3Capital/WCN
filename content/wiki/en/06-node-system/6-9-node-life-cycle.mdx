## life cycle path

<Callout type="warning">
Application → Due Diligence and Agreement → Permission Activation → Active Operation → Quarterly/Annual Review → Renewal, Upgrade, Downgrade or Exit; temporary review can be inserted for major events.
</Callout>

  <Card title="Admission stage" description="Evaluate resource authenticity, historical reputation, and overlap with existing subgraphs; request pilot tasks if necessary." icon="search" />

<Statement>Nodes are not NFT-style permanent assets, but service-type contracts bound to performance and compliance; proper design allows the network to maintain marginal quality without degradation during the expansion period.</Statement>

---

## System rationale for strict life cycle

Static expansion will cause three types of problems: **Routes are occupied by low-production nodes**, **PoB dispute processing costs increase**, and **new nodes are deterred due to old nodes occupying space** (adverse selection). Dynamic renewal implies "seat rent" as a continuing contribution, which is consistent with the logic of coverage banker's annual book review in TradFi.

<Callout type="info">
It is recommended to expose minimum transparency at the product layer: for example, node retention rates aggregated by track and changes in average closed-loop duration, so that market participants can understand whether the network is getting thicker or thinner - without revealing the commercial secrets of individual nodes.
</Callout>
