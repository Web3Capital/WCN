## Functional dimension (what to do)

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Project node" description="Carrying token economic design, online path, compliance narrative and financing milestones; the origin of the demand side." icon="package" />
  <Card title="capital node" description="Family offices, venture capital, market makers, ecological funds; provide check size, decision-making rhythm and structural preferences." icon="coins" />
  <Card title="service node" description="Law firms, auditing, security, development, branding, research; turn referrals into deliverable SOWs." icon="wrench" />
  <Card title="distribution node" description="Exchanges, media, KOL, conference organizers; undertake liquidity and market narrative." icon="radio" />
  <Card title="Regional node" description="National master node, city node; bringing global agreements to local relationships and activities." icon="globe" />
  <Card title="Agent node" description="The automated executors of bound workflows: screening, reaching, monitoring, and reporting must have SLAs and traces." icon="bot" />
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="info">
Typical composite portrait: A Tokyo-based AI venture capital can be simultaneously marked in the system as "capital node + Japan geographical anchor point + AI industry node + seat at a certain level". When matching, select subsets according to scenarios to avoid using a single label to describe business reality.
</Callout>

<Statement>PoB and settlement require knowing "who signed and endorsed what role"; multidimensional classification is the lowest-cost solution to compile real business relationships into a system executable structure.</Statement>
