## Five windows, open together for the first time

The chapter on timing sets out five conditions. Each has matured on its own before. The whitepaper's claim is that they open together for the first time.

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="One" icon="building-2">Web3 shifts from a technology race to a resource-organization race.</Card>
  <Card title="Two" icon="bot">AI agents can execute, but lack a business settlement layer.</Card>
  <Card title="Three" icon="target">The market respects results again, not excitement.</Card>
  <Card title="Four" icon="gem">RWA, stablecoins, and institutionalization make asset networks real.</Card>
  <Card title="Five" icon="wrench">The tooling is ready — the network can be built first.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Why neither earlier nor later works

A window that is open is not open forever. Stepping too early and stepping too late both fail, for opposite reasons.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Five years earlier">Institutions had thin entry, agents could not carry real tasks, and the stablecoin and custody rails were immature. The hybrid loop of off-chain work and onchain settlement could not scale.</Step>
  <Step title="Five years later">The default collaboration stack risks being locked in by large incumbents and vertical software. Once a workflow becomes the default, switching cost shifts to the defender's side.</Step>
  <Step title="Now">Agents can take on semi-structured work, the rails reduce friction, and the market pays for verifiable results. The category is open and not yet locked.</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info">
WCN's first stage is the business network and PoB density. The onchain part is an option that strengthens trust and settlement, not a precondition that must be solved before the network starts.
</Callout>

## What first-mover means here

First-mover advantage in WCN is not a head start on the concept. The concept does not protect anyone. The advantage is the record built while competitors remain fragmented on point tools.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Verified loops" icon="circle-check">Real collaborations that closed, were checked, and entered the record — proof that the network produces outcomes, not descriptions.</Card>
  <Card title="Settled records" icon="coins">Contributions attributed and settled, accumulating into a ledger that a later entrant cannot retroactively own.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="warning">
The advantage is held by running the network through the window, not by announcing it first. Concepts are easy to copy; a record of verified loops and settled outcomes is not.
</Callout>

<Statement>Timing advantage = five windows open together for the first time. The task is to run the network before they close, so the first-mover advantage takes the form of verified loops and settled records rather than concepts.</Statement>
