## Honest comparison: four representative types

Each tool below shares an overlap with WCN — data, or collaboration. The purpose and the trust model differ. WCN composes with each rather than absorbing it.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Oracles, such as Chainlink" icon="link-2">Solves: bringing off-chain data onchain — prices, events, reserve attestations. Does not solve: deal facilitation, service delivery, multi-role attribution, or business workflows. With WCN: an oracle can be part of the evidence stack when PoB references an off-chain fact. WCN does not run a price feed.</Card>
  <Card title="Indexes, such as The Graph" icon="contact">Solves: onchain data query and subgraph organization. Does not solve: structured collaboration over off-chain contracts, messages, and deliverables. With WCN: an index helps surface onchain state. WCN records and verifies the off-chain and hybrid process.</Card>
  <Card title="DAO tools, such as Snapshot and Safe" icon="landmark">Solves: proposals, voting, and multi-signature treasury. Does not solve: delivery-oriented B2B task flow, service SLAs, or contributor-style attribution. With WCN: DAO tooling leans toward governance inside one organization; WCN leans toward a business network across organizations.</Card>
  <Card title="CRM, such as Salesforce or HubSpot" icon="chart-bar">Solves: sales pipeline, customer records, and marketing automation. Does not solve: onchain settlement, contribution proof, or node-based sharing. With WCN: CRM is a layer to integrate. WCN supplies the semantics of who created verifiable value in the network.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## The structural difference in WCN

WCN places four roles on one logic chain. The difference is the loop, not any single role.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Node = responsibility unit" icon="user">Not only a user ID, but an accountable entrance for resources and performance. Reputation binds to Proof of Business (PoB).</Card>
  <Card title="Agent = execution layer" icon="settings">Enters scoped task flow and approval, not isolated chat. Output connects to evidence and attribution.</Card>
  <Card title="PoB = value entrance" icon="circle-check">A record reaches the value layer only when the result holds. It is decoupled from activity and post count.</Card>
  <Card title="Settlement = bearing layer" icon="coins">Sharing aligns with a record of contribution and long-term incentives, not a narrative.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Where the advantage sits in differentiation

The same accumulation that defends the model marks the boundary. A single CRM instance or a single DAO treasury cannot reproduce a cross-organization map.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Data">Once cross-loop attribution and evidence standards become habit, the historical record becomes the default basis for review and sharing.</Step>
  <Step title="Supply-side exclusivity">Trusted nodes and their contribution map are bound to PoB. A competitor cannot copy the loops that already happened.</Step>
  <Step title="Switching cost">Workflow, Agent configuration, and PoB reputation bind to the operating rhythm. Migration equals rebuilding the ledger.</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning">
It would overstate the case to say WCN replaces everything. The accurate statement is narrower: WCN deepens the layer of business collaboration and result verification, and completes other capabilities through integration and partners.
</Callout>

<Statement>WCN is not a better DAO or a Web3 version of Salesforce. It defines the contribution, execution, and settlement layer of a business network — adjacent to and composable with oracles, indexes, governance tools, and CRM, without overlapping their core propositions.</Statement>
