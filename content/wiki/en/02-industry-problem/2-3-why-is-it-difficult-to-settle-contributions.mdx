## There is no shortage of contributions, what is missing is provable contributions

When a project can obtain funding, it is often not driven by a single role; when a cooperation can be implemented, it is often not completed by a single node. Recommendations, research, documentation, growth execution, relationship advancement, risk management, service delivery—all impact outcomes.

<Callout type="warning">
The problem is: most of these influences stay at "everyone knows someone has done it" rather than entering into "the system can prove who actually promoted the result."
</Callout>

A real industry scenario: A project completed a $3M seed round. Introducer A initially introduced the project; Capitalist B did due diligence and led the investment; Service Provider C provided the legal structure; Market Maker D later provided liquidity commitments. How should the value of this deal be allocated among A, B, C, and D? In the current industry, the answer is: there is no standard answer - rely on relationships, rely on negotiation, and rely on voice.

---

## Four fundamental reasons

### 1. Many contributions occur before the results

Before the business closed loop is truly established, a large number of key actions have already taken place: introduction, screening, organization, preparation of materials, promotion of dialogue, and resource coordination. These actions are very important to the result, but because they are not the "last signing moment", they are often ignored by the system.

<Callout type="info">
Traditional finance uses Mandate Letter, Engagement Letter and Fee Schedule to lock in roles and allocations before the deal begins. Web3 has almost no such front-end structure—contribution happens in a gray area, and distribution happens as an afterthought.
</Callout>

### 2. Contribution leaves no structured evidence

A lot of critical collaboration in the industry still happens in private chats, phone calls, offline meetings, and ad-hoc documents. As a result, even if it occurs, many of the pushing behaviors in the process cannot be reconstructed.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="No unified event number" icon="hash">Each collaboration has no unique identifier and cannot be tracked.</Card>
  <Card title="No unified evidence package" icon="package">Contracts, minutes, reports, and emails are scattered and incomplete.</Card>
  <Card title="No unified role statement" icon="drama">There is no systematic record of who was involved and what role they played.</Card>
  <Card title="There is no unified state machine" icon="chart-bar">There is no standardized tracking of what stage an incident is at.</Card>
</CardGrid>

### 3. Volume substitution contribution

In an environment without clear recording and review mechanisms, contributions can easily be distorted by the following factors:

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Who is louder" icon="megaphone">People who are more expressive are more likely to be seen.</Card>
  <Card title="Who is closer to the center" icon="target">People with more conspicuous positions are more likely to contribute by default.</Card>
  <Card title="Who stands in the last ring" icon="flag">The last person to sign may overwrite the value of previous multi-party collaboration.</Card>
  <Card title="Who can tell stories better?" icon="file-text">Narrative ability replaces evidentiary ability.</Card>
</CardGrid>

### 4. Lack of unified attribution rules

In most collaboration scenarios, there are no clear rules to judge:

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Who is the dominant party" icon="crown">Is it the first person to introduce it, or the person who promotes it until the result is established?</Card>
  <Card title="Who are the collaborators?" icon="handshake">Which roles provide key support but do not dominate?</Card>
  <Card title="Does Agent count as a contributor?" icon="bot">If an Agent does critical work, how is its value recorded?</Card>
  <Card title="What should not be counted" icon="ban">How should verbal help, repeated claims, and fruitless actions be eliminated?</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Settlement failure will directly destroy the incentive structure

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Really valuable people will be lost" icon="door-open">If the real contribution to settlement cannot be proven in the long term, the first people to be disappointed will be those who really promote the establishment of the closed loop - they will turn to other systems with positive feedback.</Card>
  <Card title="The excitement will overwhelm the results" icon="megaphone">When the system lacks the ability to prove results, the industry is most likely to be rewarded by high-frequency vocalization, crowd-building and exposure, rather than closed-loop and delivery.</Card>
  <Card title="Collaboration is becoming increasingly short-term" icon="timer">Without clear settlement expectations, everyone is more inclined to only take actions that can be seen in front of them, rather than investing in long-term collaboration and complex promotion.</Card>
  <Card title="The network cannot be deposited into long-term credit" icon="trending-down">If every result only stays at "it was done this time" without entering the structured ledger, the entire network will not be able to form a long-term reputation and position system.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="warning">
Without proof of contribution, there will be no stable incentives; without stable incentives, there will be no high-quality network.
</Callout>

## Why proof of contribution is the core of WCN

WCN does not start from "issuing rewards", but starts from "first defining what counts as results, what counts as evidence, and what counts as effective contributions".

<Statement>
What WCN wants to solve is not "how to provide more incentives", but "how to turn behaviors that truly create value into verifiable, attributable, and settleable objects."
</Statement>

## Core conclusion

Contributions are difficult to calculate, not because there is no contribution, but because four things are missing:

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Lack of unified event structure" icon="building-2">There are no unique IDs, role declarations, and stage tracking for each collaboration.</Card>
  <Card title="Lack of credible evidence chain" icon="link">Contracts, minutes, receipts, and on-chain records are not archived in a unified manner.</Card>
  <Card title="Lack of clear attribution rules" icon="scale">There is no standard definition of Lead, Collaboration, Auxiliary, Agent contribution.</Card>
  <Card title="Lack of systematic settlement entrance" icon="coins">As a result, it cannot enter the long-term credit, PoB and value precipitation layers.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="tip">
This is why WCN’s PoB (Proof of Business) is not an additional module, but the most critical institutional foundation of the entire network.
</Callout>
