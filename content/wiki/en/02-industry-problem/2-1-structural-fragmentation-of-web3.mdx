## Many resources, few systems

There are a large number of project parties, capital parties, media, exchanges, market makers, legal affairs, security teams, technical service providers, KOLs, event organizers in the industry, and there are also more and more AI tools and automation capabilities. The problem is not that the resources do not exist, but that most of these resources still exist in a fragmented, low-structured, and strongly dependent manner.

<Callout type="warning">
There are many "points" in the industry, but there is no strong enough "network".
</Callout>

An intuitive comparison: Traditional finance has infrastructure such as Bloomberg Terminal, Pitchbook, and Dealogic to structurally connect capital with projects. The core resource routing in the Web3 industry remains Telegram group chats and business card exchanges at events.

## Reasons for four-layer split

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Participants are scattered in different circles" icon="tree-palm">Project parties, capital parties, service providers and media each have their own circles and information sources. The capital that a project can access depends on the founder's personal relationship, and the customers that a service provider can receive rely on referrals from acquaintances. Resource flows rely on individuals rather than systems.</Card>
  <Card title="Collaboration happens in ad-hoc channels" icon="message-circle">A lot of critical action still happens in private chats, group chats, phone calls, and ad-hoc shared documents. These methods can facilitate things, but it is difficult to form a long-term, structured, and reusable collaboration system.</Card>
  <Card title="Each participant works with their own standards" icon="ruler">The project side has its own material format, the capital side has its own screening criteria, and the service provider has its own delivery habits. Without a unified structured interface, even if resources are connected, they often only collide rather than collaborate.</Card>
  <Card title="Relationship network ≠ Responsibility network" icon="handshake">Web3 is good at building a network of relationships, but a network of relationships is not the same as a network of responsibilities. A lot of collaboration stops at "we got to know each other and talked about it", and rarely goes into "there is an owner, there is a process, there is delivery, and there is settlement".</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="info">
A typical scenario: A project party met 30 investors on Token2049, exchanged business cards, and added Telegram. Three months later, less than three have actually entered the deal process, and only one may have a result - not because there are no opportunities, but because there is no system to advance the opportunities into a closed loop.
</Callout>

## Quadruple inefficiency caused by fragmentation

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="It is difficult to move forward after an opportunity is discovered" icon="hourglass">What is really missing is not the introduction, but a system that turns the introduction into a process, and then turns the process into a result. Deal is not lacking in the first step. What is lacking is who can continue to follow, who can provide materials, and who can attract the right people.</Card>
  <Card title="Good resources cannot be reused by the network" icon="refresh-cw">An excellent node may be very valuable, but if its ability only remains in the individual - not recorded in a structured manner, not precipitated by processes, and not amplified by agents - in the end it is the individual's ability rather than the network's ability.</Card>
  <Card title="The result cannot be precipitated into long-term value" icon="trending-down">Cooperation is accomplished, but if there is no unified recording method, the result will only remain as "this has been done." Fuzzy memory cannot support verifiable settlement, trustworthy attribution, and continued enhancement of network effects.</Card>
  <Card title="Lively alternative system" icon="megaphone">When the system is not strong enough, what is most likely to be seen is excitement: popular narratives, active communities, high-frequency voices, and a large number of surface connections. If these cannot enter a closed loop, it will be difficult to accumulate long-term network capabilities.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## WCN must start here

If the problem of resource fragmentation is not solved first, many things will not be possible later. Therefore, the first step of WCN is not to talk about the asset layer or governance layer first, but to answer:

<Statement>
How to organize fragmented resources into a collaborative, verifiable, and sedimentable network?
</Statement>

## Core conclusion

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Resources are not organized into networks">Participants are scattered in their own circles, and connections rely on relationships rather than systems.</Card>
  <Card title="Collaboration does not enter the structural level">A large number of actions stay in private chats and temporary channels, lacking workflow.</Card>
  <Card title="There is no unified verification method for contributions">Who did what and to what extent, there is a lack of records and proof.</Card>
  <Card title="Value cannot be systematically precipitated">One-time cooperation cannot turn into long-term network assets and reputation accumulation.</Card>
</CardGrid>
