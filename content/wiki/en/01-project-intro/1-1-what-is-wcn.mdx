## WCN is a business network

Web3 and AI do not lack projects, capital, media, service providers, regional resources, or execution capacity. What they lack is a system that organizes those resources, coordinates the work, verifies the result, and settles the value.

WCN places different participants into one workflow. Collaboration closes around real business — not around traffic, attention, or a short-term narrative.

<Callout type="warning">
WCN begins with business, not with a chain. The first question is not "can this go onchain?" It is: can the resources be organized, can the result be verified, and can the value be settled?
</Callout>

## Three core components

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Global node network" icon="globe">Operators — project teams, capital partners, service providers, media, and regional organizers — who bring resources and own responsibility. A node is not an ordinary account; it is an accountable unit that routes resources, advances business, and carries risk.</Card>
  <Card title="AI agent execution layer" icon="bot">Research, sourcing, outreach, follow-up, monitoring, and coordination. Agents here do not chat; they enter scoped tasks under explicit permission and a full audit record.</Card>
  <Card title="Proof of Business (PoB)" icon="circle-check">WCN's verification mechanism. PoB is not a participation score — it is evidence of a real business outcome. Only a verifiable, traceable, reviewed result enters the record and proceeds to settlement.</Card>
  <Card title="How the three work together" icon="settings">Nodes bring resources, agents scale execution, and PoB verifies the outcome. Together they form a business network that can operate for the long term.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Minimal logic: who actually drives the outcome?

WCN does not measure surface participation. It measures five things.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Who introduced the opportunity">How a project, capital, service, or regional resource enters the system.</Step>
  <Step title="Who advanced the collaboration">Who moves an opportunity from a contact into a structured workflow.</Step>
  <Step title="Who executed the work">How people and agents turn a scoped task into a deliverable result.</Step>
  <Step title="Who left the evidence">Without evidence there is no verification; without verification there is no durable value.</Step>
  <Step title="Who made the outcome real">The measure is not excitement. It is whether the outcome actually happened.</Step>
</Steps>

## The full path, from resource to settlement

<Callout type="info">
Node routes a resource → the opportunity enters the workspace → people and agents execute → an outcome forms → evidence is submitted → PoB verifies → the entry reaches the settlement layer.
</Callout>

This path is what separates WCN from a community, a CRM, or a matching platform. It does not stop at connection; it continues through execution, verification, and record.

## Why a business network, not a chain

A project that calls itself "a chain" first tends to build the technical shell, then look for real demand. WCN reverses the order. Onchain settlement carries meaning only after three conditions hold.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Can the resources be organized?" icon="clipboard-list">If resources stay in private chats and personal relationships, there is no network.</Card>
  <Card title="Can the outcome be verified?" icon="search">If results can only be described in words, the network has no trustworthy ledger.</Card>
  <Card title="Can the value be settled?" icon="coins">If contribution is not recorded and attributed, long-term collaboration does not hold.</Card>
  <Card title="Is there real input to carry onchain?" icon="link-2">Only once the business network exists do the protocol and asset layers stop being empty technical shells.</Card>
</CardGrid>
