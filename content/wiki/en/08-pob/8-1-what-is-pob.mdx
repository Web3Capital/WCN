## PoB records a result, not participation

WCN runs on three things: a global node network, a Proof Ledger gated by PoB, and AI agents that enter tasks and leave evidence. PoB sits between work and the ledger. It does not ask who calculated fastest or who staked the most. It asks who advanced a result that an independent reviewer can confirm.

A claim of "it is done" stays verbal until evidence backs it. PoB raises the gate to a fixed standard: outcome, evidence, and attribution. Only a result that clears that gate enters the value layer the network recognizes.

<Statement>PoB does not ask who took part. It asks who advanced a business result that review can confirm.</Statement>

---

## The four meanings of PoB in the system

PoB carries four distinct functions. Each one governs a different stage between a result and its settlement.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Result threshold" icon="door-open">A result is recognized as Verified only after it passes PoB. Until then it stays at the collaboration layer and does not enter the value the system records.</Card>
  <Card title="Attribution entry" icon="puzzle">Only a result inside PoB can be split among contributors and routed into the Carry waterfall. No PoB, no defensible attribution.</Card>
  <Card title="Settlement precondition" icon="coins">PoB is the required step before long-term value distribution. No PoB, no settlement. The gate sits ahead of every payout.</Card>
  <Card title="Network memory" icon="brain">A reconciled closed loop does not vanish. It settles into a reusable system asset — a record later collaboration can cite, lowering the cost of repeated trust.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Why common Web3 incentives cannot measure real value

Most Web3 reward mechanisms optimize an observable proxy rather than a confirmed outcome. PoB exists because that gap is structural, not accidental.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Airdrops" icon="ban">Distributed by address and interaction snapshot. They reward scriptable behavior with no stable link to a business outcome.</Card>
  <Card title="Points and tasks" icon="ban">Optimize check-ins, reposts, and form fills. These actions detach easily from delivery and acceptance.</Card>
  <Card title="Pure staking yield" icon="ban">Compensates locked capital and network security, not work done for a customer or a closed deal.</Card>
  <Card title="Holdings and volume" icon="ban">Proxy figures that incentives can distort through wash activity and short-term subsidy.</Card>
</CardGrid>

PoB raises the settlement gate to outcome, evidence, and attribution. No single proxy above can stand in for that gate.

## PoB inside the WCN stack

PoB is an internal proof mechanism. Its evidence chain stays available for external audit and regulatory review — it can be examined, not self-attested.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Result threshold" icon="door-open">A result that has not passed PoB stays narrative. It does not enter the long-term value the system recognizes.</Card>
  <Card title="Attribution entry" icon="puzzle">Once evidence clears review, the split among lead, collaborating, benefiting, and reviewing roles begins.</Card>
  <Card title="Settlement precondition" icon="coins">Token and long-term equity distribution are bound to the Proof Ledger, so storytelling alone does not earn a payout.</Card>
  <Card title="Network memory" icon="brain">Sealed PoB records form a citable reputation and case library, reducing repeated diligence.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="tip">
Without PoB, "it is done" stays a spoken claim. With PoB, the same claim must arrive with a submittable, reviewable evidence package before it becomes a system fact.
</Callout>
