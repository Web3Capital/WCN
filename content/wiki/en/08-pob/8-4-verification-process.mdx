## Five Steps to Verification (Workflow View)

<Steps>
  <Step title="① Event submission">Generate a **unique event number** for the final state, and bind Deal, Task, related nodes and statement types (financing/service/growth, etc.). Submission bears the **burden of proof**: any gaps in materials must be filled by the submitting party within the time limit.</Step>
  <Step title="② Upload evidence package">Submit contracts, on-chain references, bank/escrow paths (compliance desensitization), emails/minutes, acceptance forms, etc. according to the template. **Analog to GIPS**: The data should be sufficient to allow an independent party to use reasonable efforts to review the conclusion, rather than just "trust the applicant".</Step>
  <Step title="③ Review queue">The auditor checks: **Authenticity** (whether it is consistent with a third party or on the chain), **Integrity** (whether the final state is closed), **Time Sequence** (whether it is backdated), **Attribution Boundary** (whether it conflicts with 8.5), **Exclusion Rules** (whether it falls into 8.3). Can be combined with multi-signature, rotation or sampling audits.</Step>
  <Step title="④ Conclusion generation">**Passed** / **Supplement** / **Rejected** / **Dispute Escalation**. There should be a frequency and time limit for supplementary documents to prevent endless delays; objection records should be kept for disputes to avoid "passing by silence".</Step>
  <Step title="⑤ Filing into account">After passing, the PoB event ledger (including conclusion hash, key metadata and version) is written. This record is similar to an audited performance entry: it can serve as an anchor for subsequent settlement, reputation and revalidation.</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info">
Compared with PoW: what miners prove is **computing power**; what PoB audit proves is **the consistency of materials and facts**. Comparison with PoA: PoA trusts the **certifier's identity**; PoB still requires **evidence package**, and the reviewer's identity does not replace the material.
</Callout>

## Proof Desk: Verification center, not network disk

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Collect evidence" icon="inbox">Structured field + attachment version; avoid losing files in the chat window and making it unauditable.</Card>
  <Card title="management review" icon="scale">Queue priority, SLA, double review or random inspection rules can be managed.</Card>
  <Card title="Leave room for dispute" icon="message-square">The reasons for objections and rulings are left behind to facilitate subsequent review and rule iteration.</Card>
  <Card title="Output conclusion" icon="flag">Clarify the final state: **Whether to enter PoB**; if it fails, it shall not enter the long-term value distribution semantics.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## The place where anti-cheating lies at the process level

- **Cross-check**: Are the on-chain time, third-party email domain name, and contract number consistent with public disclosures.
- **Mutually Exclusive Events**: Only one **Archived** dominant chain is allowed for the same financing round and the same acceptance target, unless split by ruling.
- **Cooling and Frequency**: Abnormally high frequency, templated materials trigger manual or algorithm-assisted review.

<Callout type="tip">
The verification process pushed WCN from "who has the loudest voice" to "who has the toughest materials." Without this step, PoB is no different from off-chain Excel recording credit, and is more susceptible to tampering.
</Callout>
