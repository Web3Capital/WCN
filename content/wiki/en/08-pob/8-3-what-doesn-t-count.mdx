## Activity that has not reached a result

Posting, reposting, group-building, an introduction with no follow-through, an intent negotiation, and "help" with no deliverable do not form a PoB object. The reason is structural: the marginal cost of copying these actions approaches zero, and none holds a stable causal chain to a business outcome. Record them, and incentives flow to volume and visibility rather than output.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Pure reach" icon="ban">Impressions, likes, and member counts on their own. Like airdrop interaction volume, they can be farmed and rarely link to delivery.</Card>
  <Card title="Introduction without a loop" icon="ban">Knowing someone is not advancing a signature or a payment. The observable result and evidence of 8.2 are still required.</Card>
  <Card title="Plans and intent" icon="ban">A letter of intent, a verbal consensus, an exclusive negotiation — none counts as a closed deal.</Card>
  <Card title="Claim without evidence" icon="ban">Claiming to have led, with no dated material and no third-party trace, means the result cannot be re-reviewed.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Pseudo-contribution and the gaming surface

Some inputs reach the result layer but distort attribution. The verification process targets each one, and escalates to a Verdict when a dispute cannot be reconciled.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Duplicate claim" icon="triangle-alert">Several parties each claim sole lead on one result. Event numbering, timestamps, and mutual-exclusion rules apply; a contested case ends in a Verdict.</Card>
  <Card title="Last-mile capture" icon="triangle-alert">A party appears only at the final step with no record at the key nodes. Attribution follows whoever cleared the blocking condition (see 8.5).</Card>
  <Card title="Volume over value" icon="triangle-alert">The same failure as a points system: what is loud is noise, and what is quiet is often the real advance.</Card>
  <Card title="Output not adopted" icon="triangle-alert">Agent or script output that no human or customer adopted is the same as undeployed code. It is not a business result.</Card>
  <Card title="Wash and recycling" icon="triangle-alert">Wash volume and circular liquidity. When evidence comes only from controllable parties with no external beneficiary, review tightens.</Card>
  <Card title="Stitched or backdated evidence" icon="triangle-alert">Timestamps are cross-checked against third-party records. Material must be complete, accurate, and not misleading; fabrication is rejected outright.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Why this differs from common Web3 rewards

Each common reward optimizes a proxy that detaches from real work. PoB raises the cost of scoring so that a real closed loop is worth more than a farmed one.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Airdrops and tasks" icon="ban">Optimize rule-programmability, not delivery quality. The cost of a fake can fall below the cost of a real customer.</Card>
  <Card title="Staking" icon="ban">Optimizes capital lock, not service completion. A large holder may close no customer loop at all.</Card>
  <Card title="Points ranking" icon="ban">Optimizes short-term action frequency, which detaches easily from real advancement.</Card>
  <Card title="The PoB response" icon="circle-check">A real closed deal outpays a high score. That is the economic core of anti-gaming, not a moral appeal.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="warning">
If "what does not count" is enforced loosely, PoB dilutes into another marketing score. Strong enforcement rests on consistent review standards and transparent records of every supplement and rejection (see 8.4).
</Callout>

<Statement>Exclusions protect the scarcity of results and the return to honest nodes. What they harm is the settlement meaning of the whole network.</Statement>
