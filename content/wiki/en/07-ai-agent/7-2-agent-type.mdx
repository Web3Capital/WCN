## The five MVP Agents

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Founder Decision Support" icon="brain">A thinking partner for the founder. It structures options, surfaces trade-offs, and drafts reasoning for review. It frames decisions; it does not make them, and its output stays advisory until a person acts on it.</Card>
  <Card title="Project Diligence" icon="search">A four-dimension first draft of project due diligence. It retrieves from permitted sources, fills the diligence checklist, and marks every claim with a source. Figures default to "to be verified"; it does not contact the project on its own.</Card>
  <Card title="Outreach and Meeting Summary" icon="mail">Draft outreach email plus meeting transcription and summary. It produces action items, owners, and follow-up drafts against a template. Nothing is sent automatically; the node reviews and sends by hand.</Card>
  <Card title="Node Support" icon="message-circle">Automated node question-and-answer and FAQ. It answers from approved material and routes anything outside its scope to a person. It does not improvise policy or speak for the node.</Card>
  <Card title="PoB Evidence Validator" icon="circle-check">A first-pass review of submitted evidence. It checks an evidence package against the required fields and flags gaps for the Proof Ledger. It pre-screens and sorts; it never finalizes a PoB record.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Why each Agent is a separate unit

A single all-purpose Agent sharing one toolset breaks the principle of least privilege. Separating the five lets the system fix, for each one, what it may see, what it may do, and what it must record.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="What each may see" icon="eye">Project Diligence reads a wide set of materials; Node Support reads approved FAQ content; the PoB Evidence Validator reads only the submitted package and the required fields.</Card>
  <Card title="What each may do" icon="wrench">Outreach and Meeting Summary drafts notes and emails; Node Support answers within approved material. No Agent may move funds or sign on its own.</Card>
  <Card title="What each must log" icon="clipboard-list">Project Diligence records its query and the retrieved fragment; Outreach records the source meeting and timestamp; the Validator records which fields it checked and what it flagged.</Card>
  <Card title="How each reaches PoB" icon="file-text">Attribution templates differ by Agent, so an adopted diligence draft and an adopted outreach note carry different Proof fields and do not collide at settlement.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## The expansion schedule

The five MVP Agents are the starting set, not the ceiling. The whitepaper sets a phased path: the cluster grows to ten Agents in Phase 1, twenty-five in Phase 2, and forty mature Agents in Phase 3.

<Statement>Each new Agent enters the same way as the first five: a defined position, a scoped permission set, an output log, and an attribution record. Growth adds units to the cluster; it does not loosen the boundary.</Statement>

## The resident management cluster

Beneath the MVP Agents, WCN's own operation runs on the founder plus eight resident Agents. One acts as conductor, six cover the pillar domains of legal, capital, network, knowledge, brand, and operations, and two are cross-cutting: one maintains the base layer, one handles audit.

Each resident Agent carries one forbidden act and one always-true ledger assertion. The capital Agent never moves funds; the brand Agent never publishes without authorization. The pattern is the same as the MVP Agents: a fixed scope, a fixed limit, and a record that holds.

<Callout type="warning">
Without separation, permissions widen, logs cannot be defended, and a PoB record cannot be priced. The type of an Agent is a precondition for governance and settlement, not a label.
</Callout>

## One event, several Agents

<Steps>
  <Step title="Project Diligence">Reads public financing news and the internal memo, then produces a list of conflict points and open questions.</Step>
  <Step title="Founder Decision Support">Structures the trade-offs from that list and drafts the reasoning for the next round of discussion, for the founder to review.</Step>
  <Step title="Outreach and Meeting Summary">Writes the meeting conclusion as action items with owners and due dates, and attaches them to the task.</Step>
  <Step title="PoB Evidence Validator">Checks the resulting evidence package against the required fields and flags any gap before the Proof Ledger reviews it.</Step>
</Steps>

<Statement>The point of separating the Agents is to answer one question when something goes wrong: was it the model, the tools, the configuration, or the human adoption. A boundary can be drawn only once the work is split.</Statement>
