## The main path into the loop

<Callout type="info">
A task is dispatched with its type and acceptance criteria. The Agent executes within its authorization. The structured output is stored. The node owner adopts, rejects, or revises it. The state machine advances the deal or task. The result evidence is submitted. The Proof Ledger reviews it. A PoB record is generated. The entry reaches settlement and the record.
</Callout>

<Statement>The Agent's value is as a traceable driver of results. The system separates "ten pages were generated" from "three were adopted and led to a meeting held, a term changed, or a payment condition met".</Statement>

## What Agent output needs to reach Proof

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Bound to a task" icon="link">Each output is tied to a task handle. Loose results drifting in private chats or offline documents are not eligible.</Card>
  <Card title="Input and version recorded" icon="clipboard-list">The model name and version, the parameters, the retrieved fragment, and the key attachment hash — enough to reproduce the output and to collect evidence in a dispute.</Card>
  <Card title="Human adoption recorded" icon="circle-check">An explicit event: who adopted it, when, and any revision. Unlike an implicit copy-and-paste, this is structured and kept.</Card>
  <Card title="Linked to a result" icon="refresh-cw">Traceable to a deal, a milestone, or a business event. Without that link, attribution and settlement cannot be done.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="warning">
If any condition is missing, the output is treated as tool-generated and is not counted into PoB. The reason matches the one in accounting: an item without a reference and a working paper cannot enter the books.
</Callout>

## Three worked examples

<Steps>
  <Step title="Diligence material">Task: the risk section of a Series A package. Project Diligence pulls comparable risk disclosures and drafts the section with sources. The node revises and adopts it, then uploads the revised file hash. Proof links it to the final draft used in the investor meeting, and a PoB record follows once the meeting closes.</Step>
  <Step title="Evidence review">An evidence package is submitted for a completed task. The PoB Evidence Validator checks it against the required fields and flags two gaps. The node fills the gaps, and only the completed package goes to the Proof Ledger; the Validator's first pass does not pass it on its own.</Step>
  <Step title="Monitoring follow-up">A monitoring note reports an anomaly. Outreach and Meeting Summary drafts an internal task note. The risk owner adopts one and creates a follow-up task. The receipt enters the evidence package, and the entry moves to PoB once Proof passes.</Step>
</Steps>

## The counter-case: busy, with no loop

- An open, unbounded run produces many notes with no task and no owner. That is noise.
- An Agent drafts a polished email that is not sent from a WCN task and carries no adoption tag. That cannot be attributed.
- An Agent suggests a step, the node forwards it by word of mouth, and the system holds no state. That cannot be verified.

## What this step decides

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Tool, or execution layer" icon="bot">Agent work becomes the network's execution layer only after it enters the loop. Before that, it is no different from any model wrapper.</Card>
  <Card title="Settleable, or not" icon="coins">Without a Proof chain there is no PoB, and incentives and shares have no evidence to rest on.</Card>
  <Card title="Manageable, or not" icon="shield">Adoption rate, reverted adoptions, and model comparisons can be measured, so an Agent configuration can be upgraded or suspended on evidence.</Card>
  <Card title="The WCN difference" icon="zap">The point is not that it connects to a model. It is that machine output, across a multi-node business network, reaches one shared language of evidence and settlement.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="tip">
A test for acceptance: in a dispute, can the logs explain in half an hour who asked the Agent to do what, what was adopted, and what the final business result was. If they can, the loop is sound.
</Callout>
