## Short term: access and friction costs

The following are **illustrative magnitudes** for internal ROI discussions within the organization; subject to the actual contract and current network parameters.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Structured Deal Contact" icon="refresh-cw">The same project is presented with a unified data model in the system, and the capital side can often compress the initial screening time from tens of hours/week on scattered channels to a systematic queue + agent summary (the specific ratio varies depending on the size of the team and the popularity of the track).</Card>
  <Card title="Task and supplier orchestration" icon="handshake">Service and distribution nodes reduce the cold start cost of "finding the right person"; Task-level SLA and acceptance standards reduce the probability of disputes - equivalent to a built-in lightweight SOW.</Card>
  <Card title="Official participation position" icon="clipboard-list">Obtain **nomination priority** (subject to seat cap and KPI) in joint roadshows, institute-level matchmaking, joint research and other activities, which is different from the public registration channel.</Card>
  <Card title="Brand and trust transfer" icon="medal">The audited "WCN Node" joint logo (subject to brand guidelines) can be used externally; it can be compared with a **verifiable third-party endorsement** during institutional due diligence to reduce the initial trust cost of the counterparty.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Mid-term: Data and collaboration assets

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="network level signaling" icon="radio">Anonymized or aggregated track popularity, capital preference migration, and service bottleneck indicators - similar to the market screen on the terminal, but focusing on the supply and demand within the private collaboration network.</Card>
  <Card title="Reusable collaboration relationships" icon="globe">Reuse verified node combinations across Deals (such as fixing the "Legal + Audit + Market Making" package) to shorten the TTM (time-to-market) of subsequent projects.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Long term: location and settlement

As the node base increases, the scarcity of seats in the same segment increases; early high-performing nodes generally enjoy higher weights in matching sorting, co-branding, and incentive pools (mechanism details are subject to governance documents).

<Statement>The greatest long-term benefit is not "one more logo", but ** a stable and quotable contribution track in PoB and Settlement ** - providing third-party verifiable records for subsequent financing, mergers and acquisitions, and joint fund establishment.</Statement>

---

## The value will not be automatically transferred to the account

<Callout type="warning">Nodes that fail to deliver, refuse evidence, frequently bounce tickets, or are triggered by compliance will face authority downgrade, incentive weight reset to zero or removal - similar to the cancellation of market maker discounts by exchanges and the freezing of accounts by terminal manufacturers. The ** position is an option, and execution is the exercise of the option. **</Callout>

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Output driver" icon="zap">Deal advancement, Task delivery on time, capital delivery or equivalent results.</Card>
  <Card title="Symmetrical responsibilities" icon="scroll">The expansion of authority needs to be matched by higher frequency of disclosure and review.</Card>
  <Card title="PoB threshold" icon="circle-check">Entering the settlement flow requires an evidence package approved by the Proof Desk and dispute clearance.</Card>
  <Card title="Compound interest conditions" icon="trending-up">Only those who meet the standards for multiple consecutive quarters can enter the fast track for upgrade and renewal (the specific number of quarters depends on the policy).</Card>
</CardGrid>
