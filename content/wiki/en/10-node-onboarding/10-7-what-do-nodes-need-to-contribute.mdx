## Four types of hard contributions

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Resource entry (Input)" icon="package">The available resources are regularly updated by seat type: the capital side is the investment amount and decision-making cycle; the service side is the available working hours and delivery templates; the distribution side is the schedule and flow/depth commitment; the regional side is the activity and institutional reach list. **"Business card style" empty posting is prohibited. **</Card>
  <Card title="Closed-loop propulsion (Throughput)" icon="refresh-cw">Respond to matches, claim tasks, and promote milestones (term sheets, audit delivery, listing materials, etc.) within the agreed time limit. Common operational expectations: Active nodes** participate in at least N auditable paths** every quarter (N is determined by the seat type and agreement, often ≥1).</Card>
  <Card title="Evidence and Disclosure (Evidence)" icon="file-text">Deliverables, email minutes, on-chain hashes, signed SOW, etc. enter the Evidence Packet; major conflicts of interest must be declared in advance. Bidding investment banks: working paper thinking rather than verbal confirmation.</Card>
  <Card title="Accept review (Governance)" icon="search">Cooperate with KPI review, random inspection, compliance questionnaire and address rotation; accept **temporary freezing of operation rights** during the dispute period. Refusal to review is regarded as one of the triggering conditions for a major breach of contract.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Why must it be written into protocols and systems

<Callout type="warning">If nodes only enjoy exposure and data but do not undertake delivery, the **Signal-to-Noise Ratio** and **PoB Credibility** of the network will decrease simultaneously - ultimately damaging all contract-keeping nodes. High-quality networks rely on verification loops to expand capacity, not capitation fees.</Callout>

## KPI pointing by type (example)

| Node types | Typical KPI dimensions | Common red lines |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Capital | Response time, due diligence ratio, closing rate of delivery or clear rejection | Long-term zero response, malicious price reduction and breach of contract |
| Service | On-time delivery rate, re-inspection pass rate, customer satisfaction sampling | Repeated overdues, serious discrepancies between delivery and promotion |
| Distribution / exchange / market making | Agreed exposure or depth compliance rate, compliance disclosure | False traffic, market manipulation, undisclosed interests |
| Region | Number of events, number of institutions introduced, localization support tickets | Exclusive seats but no online activities organized |

The specific thresholds are subject to the signed version of the agreement and the current operation manual; the above table is used to **align expectations** and is not a unilateral commitment.

## Renewal, upgrade, downgrade and cancellation

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Renew" icon="repeat">Rolling review: no open matters for compliance + KPI compliance + annual fee/seat fee (if any) settled. Analogy Professional Association Membership Annual Report.</Card>
  <Card title="upgrade" icon="arrow-up">If you have high performance for multiple consecutive periods and no major disputes, you can apply for a wider area, track or operation rights; safety and legal signatures are required.</Card>
  <Card title="Downgrade" icon="arrow-down">Insufficient output or decreased risk control score: reduce the scope of visible deals, turn off operation rights, and keep them read-only.</Card>
  <Card title="Clear out" icon="circle-x">Major violations, fraud, sanctions hit or repeated rejection of evidence and review: terminate the agreement, revoke NFT mapping, and announce partners within the necessary scope (to the extent permitted by law).</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Statement>The vitality of the node system comes from the **executable exit mechanism** - cutting off the "only in but not out" social group can protect long-term network capital.</Statement>
