## The four duties

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Bring resources" icon="package">Keep callable resources current by seat: a capital node's check size and decision cycle; a service node's available hours and delivery templates; a distribution node's schedule and reach; a regional node's events and institutional contacts. Empty placeholder listings are not accepted.</Card>
  <Card title="Advance collaboration" icon="refresh-cw">Respond to matches, claim tasks, and move milestones — term sheets, audit delivery, listing materials — within the agreed window. An active node advances at least one auditable path each quarter.</Card>
  <Card title="Submit evidence" icon="file-text">Deliverables, meeting minutes, onchain hashes, and signed scopes enter the evidence package. A material conflict of interest is declared in advance. The standard is working-paper evidence, not verbal confirmation.</Card>
  <Card title="Comply" icon="search">Accept KPI review, spot checks, the compliance questionnaire, and address rotation. Accept a temporary freeze of operation rights during a dispute. A refusal to be reviewed is a material breach.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Why the duties are written into the protocol

<Callout type="warning">If nodes take exposure and data but carry no delivery, the network's signal-to-noise ratio and the credibility of PoB both fall, which harms every node that keeps its duties. A high-quality network grows through verified outcomes, not through a head-count fee.</Callout>

## KPI focus by node type

| Node type | Typical KPI focus | Common red lines |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Capital | Response time, due-diligence rate, rate of closing or clear rejection | Sustained non-response; bad-faith repricing or breach |
| Service | On-time delivery rate, re-inspection pass rate, satisfaction sampling | Repeated delays; delivery that diverges from the brief |
| Distribution / market making | Agreed reach or depth compliance, compliance disclosure | False traffic; market manipulation; undisclosed interest |
| Region | Events held, institutions introduced, localization support | An exclusive seat with no activity organized |

The thresholds follow the signed agreement and the current operating manual. The table aligns expectations; it is not a unilateral commitment.

## Renewal, upgrade, downgrade, and exit

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Renew" icon="repeat">A rolling review: no open compliance matters, KPIs met, and seat fees settled where they apply.</Card>
  <Card title="Upgrade" icon="arrow-up">Sustained performance and no major dispute can support a wider region, track, or operation right. Security and legal sign-off is required.</Card>
  <Card title="Downgrade" icon="arrow-down">Insufficient output or a lower risk-control score narrows visible deals and removes operation rights, leaving read access.</Card>
  <Card title="Exit" icon="circle-x">A material breach, fraud, a sanctions hit, or repeated refusal of evidence ends the agreement and revokes the NFT mapping, disclosed to partners within the limits the law allows.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Statement>The node system stays alive through an enforceable exit. Removing the seat that only enters and never leaves is what protects long-term network capital.</Statement>
