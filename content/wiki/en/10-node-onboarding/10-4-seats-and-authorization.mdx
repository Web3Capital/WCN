# The seat is the position; the authorization is the boundary.

## Four seat axes

A seat can combine axes. Together they fix the workflow template and the KPI category that apply to a node.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Function seat" icon="settings">The principal role: capital, project, service, distribution, region, or agent. It sets the default workflow and KPI category.</Card>
  <Card title="Region seat" icon="globe">Country, city, or time-zone coverage. Country and city seats carry exclusivity, with one partner per market, to prevent internal competition and reputation dilution.</Card>
  <Card title="Track seat" icon="tag">A domain such as AI, RWA, payments, or infrastructure. It drives match weighting and eligibility for joint activity.</Card>
  <Card title="Task seat" icon="target">Bound to a specific deal, task, or activity. Permissions are time-boxed and recovered or reduced when the task ends.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Four authorization levels

Authorization is symmetric with responsibility. Each level grants reach and, with it, a matching duty.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Access (read)" icon="eye">Which deal rooms, document versions, and metrics are visible. It can be scoped to the field level on a need-to-know basis.</Card>
  <Card title="Participation (engage)" icon="door-open">Bid, comment, claim tasks, and join the meeting sequence. A conflict declaration is usually required first.</Card>
  <Card title="Operation (act)" icon="wrench">Onchain or API actions: mint credentials, trigger an allocation, invoke an agent batch. Double review or a time lock is usually required.</Card>
  <Card title="Settlement eligibility (settle)" icon="coins">The condition for entering PoB attribution and the Carry waterfall: a complete evidence package, the reviewer's signature, and no open dispute. If unmet, permissions can be frozen while the seat remains.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="info">The seat answers "what institutional responsibility does this node represent." The authorization answers "what can it move this round." Upgrading a seat does not automatically widen authorization; compliance and security review applies first.</Callout>

## Why the network depends on this layer

Without seats and authorization, the collaboration map cannot be automated. Match logic, risk control, and the audit record would lose their subject. WCN records not "who knows whom" but who did what, to which object, at what time, under which authorization.

<Statement>Seat, authorization, and evidence chain form the smallest structure that scales at the institutional level. Remove one, and favors and verbal promises return.</Statement>
