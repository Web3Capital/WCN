## Structural issues with current Web3 services

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="fragmentation" icon="smartphone">Find one for legal affairs, one for audit, one for market making, and one for growth. Each company communicates independently and there is no unified coordination interface. A project team may manage 8 Telegram groups and 5 Google Docs at the same time.</Card>
  <Card title="No standard delivery" icon="clipboard-list">The service provider says "it's done", but there is no standardized delivery confirmation mechanism. The quality of audit reports varied, the coverage of legal opinions was unclear, and the effects of growth services were not attributed.</Card>
  <Card title="No billing attribution" icon="banknote">Service fees are typically upfront or milestone payments that are not tied to final business results. Market makers took fees but liquidity did not improve, and the growth team took fees but users did not grow - there was no system to bind services and results.</Card>
  <Card title="No path to scale" icon="ruler">A good FA, lawyer, auditor's time is limited. Their methodologies cannot be replicated, resulting in service capabilities being limited by individual production capacity.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## How WCN's execution and service layers work

<Steps>
  <Step title="Service registration">Service node entry capabilities: legal affairs (contract/structure/compliance), auditing (smart contract/finance), security, brand, growth, development, research, etc. The capability enters the Service Registry and can be called by Deal.</Step>
  <Step title="Task assignment">When a Deal requires service, the system matches the appropriate service node from the Service Registry. Each Task has a clear owner, scope, deadline and acceptance criteria.</Step>
  <Step title="Execution advancement">Human service nodes and Agents advance collaboratively. Agents are responsible for information sorting, reminders, minutes and status tracking, while humans are responsible for judgment, delivery and signature.</Step>
  <Step title="Delivery and evidence">After the service is completed, the deliverables enter the Evidence Packet - legal opinions, audit reports, delivery memo, SOW confirmation, etc. Delivery quality is reviewed by Reviewers.</Step>
</Steps>

## Participants at this level

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Legal / Compliance" icon="scale">SAFE/SAFT structure review, Token legal advice, entity establishment, regulatory consulting, investor KYC/AML. In Web3, legal affairs are the link that most affects the speed of deal - a legal opinion may block the entire financing for 2 weeks.</Card>
  <Card title="Audit/Security" icon="lock">Smart contract audit, financial audit, security assessment, penetration testing. High-quality auditing is a prerequisite for projects to obtain investment and be listed on the stock exchange.</Card>
  <Card title="development/research" icon="laptop">Technology development, architecture review, research reports, competitive product analysis, and Token economic design. Research Agent can cover 80% of information organization work.</Card>
  <Card title="Growth / BD" icon="rocket">Brand strategy, content creation, community building, KOL cooperation, user acquisition. Growth Agent can standardize distribution processes and attribution tracking.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Agent's role in the execution layer

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Research Agent" icon="microscope">Project summary, competitive product analysis, risk marking, and market data compilation. Compress the analyst's 3 days of work into 30 minutes, and adopt it after human review.</Card>
  <Card title="Execution Agent" icon="zap">Automatic generation of minutes, to-do tracking, reminders, material collection, and status updates. Eliminate 30-40% of coordination time in Deal.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Statement>Agent does not replace the professional judgment of service nodes, but amplifies their production capacity - allowing the methodology of an excellent lawyer to serve 10 Deals instead of 3.</Statement>

<Callout type="tip">
The execution and service layer is located after capital and before distribution. It is the key bridge that most directly determines "whether the deal can be completed." Without this layer, WCN is just a premium version of the introductory platform.
</Callout>
