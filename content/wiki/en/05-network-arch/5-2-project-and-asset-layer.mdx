## The core question answered at this level: Why does the network exist?

Only when projects and assets enter the system with real needs can the network have collaborative meaning. Requirements are not abstract traffic, but concrete problems:

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Financing needs" icon="coins">Capital and structural support are needed - the matching of different financing tools such as seed round, Series A, strategic round, OTC, Token Warrant, etc.</Card>
  <Card title="growth demand" icon="trending-up">Requires brand, media, channel and community support - from narrative building to user acquisition, from KOL cooperation to exchange listing.</Card>
  <Card title="Service needs" icon="wrench">It requires professional service support such as legal affairs, security audit, technology development, and Token economic design.</Card>
  <Card title="Asset scenario" icon="building-2">RWA assets, on-chain equity, liquidity products and other structured assets that need to be organized, distributed and settled.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Current pain points of Web3 Deal Flow

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Information fragmentation" icon="smartphone">The project team's deck, data, and team information are scattered in Telegram, Email, Google Drive, and various meetings. A VC may receive different versions of the same project from 5 channels at the same time.</Card>
  <Card title="Very low conversion rate" icon="trending-down">Top VCs look at 3,000-5,000 projects a year and ultimately invest in 10-15 projects, with a conversion rate of less than 0.5%. A lot of time is wasted on mismatched projects - lack of structured pre-screening.</Card>
  <Card title="middleman dependence" icon="handshake">The path from the project side to the capital side is highly dependent on personal relationships and intermediaries. If a good project doesn’t know the “right people”, it may not be able to reach the right investors at all.</Card>
  <Card title="No standardized entry" icon="clipboard-list">Different VCs require information in different formats - some require Notion, some require PDF, and some require filling out forms. Project parties spend a lot of time preparing the same information repeatedly.</Card>
</CardGrid>

> **Data Reference**: According to Pitchbook 2024 data, the average cycle time for Crypto VC from first contact to completed investment is 67 days. About 40% of that time is spent on information collection and synchronization—exactly what the WCN project and asset layer are meant to solve.

## How to solve WCN: structured entry

When a project enters WCN, standardized entry is completed through Project Intake:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Basic information">Project name, track, stage, team, product status, and chain deployment status.</Step>
  <Step title="Financing information">Target amount, valuation range, financing tools (SAFE/SAFT/Token Warrant/Equity), existing investors.</Step>
  <Step title="Requirements list">Clearly list the types of resources needed—capital, legal, audit, growth, market making, listings, etc.</Step>
  <Step title="Material package">Deck, Tokenomics, technical documents, audit reports, etc. are uploaded at one time, and all capital parties see the same structured information.</Step>
</Steps>

## Typical project types entering the system

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Web3 Protocol / DApp" icon="globe">DeFi protocol, NFT platform, GameFi, SocialFi and other application layer products. Financing needs + growth needs are the main factors.</Card>
  <Card title="AI + Crypto" icon="bot">AI Agent framework, decentralized reasoning, data market, etc. Mainly technical needs + strategic capital needs.</Card>
  <Card title="RWA / Asset Project" icon="landmark">Real estate tokenization, bond on-chain, supply chain finance, etc. Mainly compliance needs + capital allocation needs.</Card>
  <Card title="infrastructure" icon="settings">L1/L2, cross-chain bridge, oracle, DePIN, payment, etc. Technical cooperation + ecological capital demand is the main focus.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="tip">
The project and asset layer are the starting point of the entire WCN architecture - no real demand enters the system, there are no assignable objects in the capital layer, no executable objects in the service layer, no propagable objects in the distribution layer, and no verifiable objects in the verification layer. It all starts here.
</Callout>
