## The question this layer answers: why does the network exist?

The network has a purpose only when projects and assets enter with real needs. A need is not abstract attention. It is a concrete problem stated clearly enough to act on.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Financing need" icon="coins">Capital and structural support — matching across instruments such as a seed round, a strategic round, OTC, or a token warrant.</Card>
  <Card title="Growth need" icon="trending-up">Brand, media, channel, and community support — from narrative to user acquisition, from KOL collaboration to an exchange listing.</Card>
  <Card title="Service need" icon="wrench">Professional support such as legal work, security audit, technical development, and token-economic design.</Card>
  <Card title="Asset scenario" icon="building-2">RWA assets, onchain equity, and liquidity products — structured assets that need to be organized, distributed, and settled.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Why fragmented deal flow stalls

The first contact rarely fails. The follow-through does. Most opportunities stall before they ever reach a structured process.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Information is scattered" icon="smartphone">A team's deck, data, and details sit across chats, email, and shared drives. The same capital partner may receive several inconsistent versions of one project through different channels.</Card>
  <Card title="The path depends on relationships" icon="handshake">Reaching capital depends on who a founder already knows. A strong project that lacks the right contacts may never reach the right investors at all.</Card>
  <Card title="No standard intake" icon="clipboard-list">Each capital partner asks for information in its own format. A project team prepares the same material repeatedly, in a new shape every time.</Card>
  <Card title="A relationship is not a process" icon="hourglass">An introduction is made, and then no owner, no scope, and no deadline follow. The opportunity exists; the system to carry it through does not.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="info">
A common case: a team meets dozens of investors at an event, swaps contacts, and follows up by chat. Months later, only a few have entered a real process, and perhaps one produces a result. The opportunities were there. The structure to carry them was not.
</Callout>

## How WCN solves it: structured intake

When a project enters WCN, intake captures the same structured information once, and every capital partner sees the same record.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Basic information">Project name, track, stage, team, product status, and chain deployment.</Step>
  <Step title="Financing information">Target amount, valuation range, instrument (SAFE, SAFT, token warrant, or equity), and existing investors.</Step>
  <Step title="Requirements list">The resources actually needed — capital, legal, audit, growth, market making, or a listing — stated plainly.</Step>
  <Step title="Material package">Deck, tokenomics, technical documents, and audit reports, uploaded once, so every capital partner reads identical, structured information.</Step>
</Steps>

## What enters the system

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Protocols and dApps" icon="globe">DeFi protocols, NFT platforms, GameFi, and SocialFi. Financing and growth are usually the leading needs.</Card>
  <Card title="AI and crypto" icon="bot">Agent frameworks, decentralized inference, and data markets. Technical and strategic-capital needs lead.</Card>
  <Card title="RWA and asset projects" icon="landmark">Real-estate tokenization, onchain bonds, and supply-chain finance. Compliance and capital-allocation needs lead.</Card>
  <Card title="Infrastructure" icon="settings">L1 and L2 networks, bridges, oracles, DePIN, and payments. Technical collaboration and ecosystem capital lead.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="tip">
The project and asset layer is the starting point of the architecture. Without real demand, the capital layer has nothing to allocate, the service layer nothing to execute, the distribution layer nothing to carry, and the verification layer nothing to prove. Everything begins here.
</Callout>
