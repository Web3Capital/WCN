## The first 90 days

The opening quarter sets the foundation: the founder's key decisions, the legal entity, the first signed nodes, and the first closed PoB. Each milestone is a checkpoint a reviewer can verify.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Day 14 · Five founder decisions">Five key decisions are settled: the GP role, the Agent downgrade, US blocking, the node candidate list, and the founder narrative.</Step>
  <Step title="Day 30 · Foundation and Track Record">Foundation registration begins, and the Track Record Pack is complete.</Step>
  <Step title="Day 60 · First nodes and counsel">The first 10 nodes are signed, General Counsel hiring is underway, and the Foundation Charter v0.1 is drafted.</Step>
  <Step title="Day 90 · First PoB and whitepaper">The first PoB loop closes — a nameable deal from the founder's existing pipeline — and whitepaper v2.1 is published.</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info">The Day 90 PoB draws on a deal already in the founder's pipeline. It closes a real, nameable outcome rather than a projected one, which is what makes the milestone verifiable.</Callout>

## Why milestone-gated, not calendar-driven

Time passing does not prove that the network works. A roadmap that turns pages on a fixed schedule pressures the team to overpromise to keep the schedule.

<Callout type="warning">A phase exits on evidence, not on a date. WCN advances when its exit criteria hold over a sustained window, which keeps the plan honest and gives reviewers a basis to check.</Callout>

## The gate between each phase

Each phase carries an exit threshold. WCN advances only when the threshold holds.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Phase 1 → Phase 2" icon="circle-check">Requires 30+ Founding nodes and 5+ verified PoB records. This confirms a real supply side and a loop that produces verified outcomes.</Card>
  <Card title="Phase 2 → Phase 3" icon="ruler">Requires 200+ nodes and a documented Token decision. This confirms the network scaled and that any Token follows a deliberate choice.</Card>
  <Card title="Phase 3 exit" icon="landmark">Requires 1,000+ nodes and compliance across five jurisdictions. This confirms scale and a regulatory base before full decentralization.</Card>
  <Card title="Sustained, not a single month" icon="calendar">A threshold counts when it holds across a window, not in one reporting period. A single-month spike does not open a gate.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## The go/no-go review

<Callout type="tip">Review the roadmap each quarter. Compare progress against the exit criteria, then decide stay, advance, or return to strengthen. When a criterion is unmet, record the reason — demand, product, compliance, or organization.</Callout>

## Milestone-gated versus one-time launch

| Dimension | Milestone-gated (WCN) | One-time full launch |
|------|----------------|----------------|
| Failure diagnosis | Traceable to nodes, outcomes, or compliance | Often ambiguous |
| Trust | Each phase has reviewable outputs | Rests on narrative and time |
| Token timing | Phase 3, after compliance | Front-loaded, before demand |
| Regulatory posture | Boundaries tighten as the network grows | Easy to overstep at once |

<Statement>Phase-gating exists to prevent self-deception inside WCN and to give reviewers a verifiable rhythm outside it.</Statement>
