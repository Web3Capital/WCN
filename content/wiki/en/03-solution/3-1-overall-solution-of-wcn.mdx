## Why existing tools do not solve the problem

Consider how the industry works today. A typical cross-border deal — a Southeast Asian DeFi project seeking Middle Eastern capital — involves six to ten roles: the project team, the financial advisor, local business development, legal counsel, media, and a market maker. Their toolchain runs across separate, disconnected surfaces.

> Telegram chat → shared documents → email follow-up → offline meeting → multisig payment → an announcement in a group

The problem with this chain is not slow execution. It is a structural gap. Each tool below covers one slice and leaves the rest untraceable.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="DAO tooling" icon="landmark">Snapshot and Tally resolve onchain voting. They do not advance a deal, allocate tasks, or attribute contribution. Governance and business execution are separate problems.</Card>
  <Card title="Matching platforms" icon="handshake">Token Terminal and RootData supply data and lists. They stop at the introduction. Execution, verification, and settlement all happen elsewhere and leave no record.</Card>
  <Card title="AI chat tools" icon="message-circle">ChatGPT and Perplexity can research. The output enters no formal workflow. There is no permission boundary, no log, no attribution, and no claim to settlement.</Card>
  <Card title="CRM and project tools" icon="chart-bar">Notion, Airtable, and HubSpot manage process. They do not model multi-party collaboration, cross-border settlement, or proof of contribution.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="warning">
The gap is not a better tool. It is collaborative infrastructure that puts every role, every action, and every result into one system. Traditional finance has Bloomberg, PitchBook, and DealCloud spanning data to execution to settlement. Web3 has no equivalent.
</Callout>

## The WCN architecture

WCN runs on three things working as one: a global, multi-tier node network; a Proof Ledger that uses Proof of Business as its settlement threshold; and AI agents that enter tasks and leave evidence. Settlement closes the loop.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Node network" icon="globe">Project teams, capital partners, service providers, media, and regional organizers, arranged as global multi-tier nodes. Each node holds a defined position, responsibility, and right. A node is not a follower; it routes resources, advances business, and carries risk.</Card>
  <Card title="AI agent execution" icon="bot">Agents are not chatbots. They are execution units inside the formal task flow. Each agent holds a capability manifest, a task contract for each assignment, and a Proof of Business record. No agent signs, transfers, or commits without human endorsement.</Card>
  <Card title="Proof of Business" icon="circle-check">Proof of Business is not a participation score. It is the verification mechanism for a real business outcome: who introduced the resource, which step advanced, what result formed, and whether the evidence passed review. Only a verified outcome reaches the value layer.</Card>
  <Card title="Settlement" icon="scale">A verified outcome enters settlement and produces traceable attribution and distribution. This resolves one of the oldest questions in Web3 — who is owed how much — by record rather than by negotiation.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## The minimal business loop

The architecture runs end to end inside a single deal. The whitepaper defines this path directly.

<Callout type="info">
A node routes a resource → the opportunity enters the workspace as structured work → tasks split across people and agents → execution produces an observable result → evidence is submitted → Proof of Business reconciles it → a Verification Node is generated → the record enters Carry settlement.
</Callout>

The smallest verifiable unit in WCN is not a post, a group, or a surface activity metric. It is one verified business loop.

## Why one workflow is the point

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Attribution becomes record" icon="search">After a traditional deal closes, who sourced it, who advanced it, and who delivered rests on assertion. WCN records the contribution chain of each role as the work happens.</Card>
  <Card title="AI enters the loop" icon="bot">An agent is no longer a standalone tool. It works inside one system, its output enters the Proof Ledger, and a human reviews it before attribution. That is how AI enters the business loop.</Card>
  <Card title="Information asymmetry narrows" icon="radio">Participants see the same data, the same progress, and the same evidence. The gray areas — "I thought you did it," "he says he introduced them" — close.</Card>
  <Card title="The network compounds" icon="trending-up">Each verified loop becomes a record. Over time, node reputation sharpens and matching improves. The system grows harder to replace — something a single-point tool cannot reach.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="tip">
WCN is not a new platform. It is a new way to organize business. For the first time, cross-border, multi-party, multi-step Web3 collaboration runs end to end inside one system, from resource entry to value settlement.
</Callout>
