<Statement>The overall solution of WCN is to gather the fragmented collaboration that was originally scattered in Telegram, Email, Google Docs, and multi-signature wallets into a unified system with identity, process, evidence, and settlement.</Statement>

## Why existing solutions don’t solve the problem

Before entering into WCN’s solution, let’s look at “how the industry works” now: A typical Web3 cross-border deal—for example, a Southeast Asian DeFi project looking for Middle Eastern capital—involves 6-10 roles such as the project party, FA, local BD, legal affairs, KOL, and market maker. Their collaboration toolchain today is:

> Telegram group chat → Google Docs sharing → Email follow-up → Offline meeting → Multi-signature wallet payment → Announcement in WeChat group

The problem with this chain is not low efficiency, but a **systemic lack**:

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="DAO tools" icon="landmark">Snapshot, Tally, etc. solve on-chain voting, but do not solve deal promotion, task allocation and contribution attribution. DAO governance and business execution are two completely different issues.</Card>
  <Card title="Matchmaking platform" icon="handshake">TokenTerminal, RootData, etc. provide data and lists, but they stop at "introduction". Subsequent execution, verification, and settlement all occur outside the platform and cannot be traced.</Card>
  <Card title="AI chat tool" icon="message-circle">ChatGPT and Perplexity can do research, but the output does not enter any formal workflow. No permission boundaries, no logs, no attribution, no billing eligibility.</Card>
  <Card title="CRM/Project Management" icon="chart-bar">Notion, Airtable, and HubSpot can manage processes, but do not understand Web3's multi-party collaboration, cross-border settlement, and contribution certification requirements.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="warning">
What the industry really lacks is not a better tool, but a set of collaborative infrastructure that can put all roles, all actions, and all results into the same system. TradFi has Bloomberg + Pitchbook + DealCloud covering the entire link from data to execution to settlement; Web3 has no equivalent so far.
</Callout>

## WCN’s four-layer solution

WCN’s answer is a four-layer unified architecture - not a bricolage of functions, but a continuous workflow from resources to settlement:

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="The first layer: node network" icon="globe">Organize project parties, capital parties, service providers, media, and regional resources into global multi-level nodes. Each node has a clear position, responsibility and authority. The node is not a fan - it is a collaborative unit that has resources, can advance, and is responsible.</Card>
  <Card title="Second layer: AI Agent execution" icon="bot">Agent is not a chatbot, but an execution layer that enters the formal task flow. The Research Agent does project screening, the Matching Agent does resource matching, and the Growth Agent does growth distribution - each Agent has permission boundaries, output logs, and attribution records.</Card>
  <Card title="The third level: Proof of Business" icon="circle-check">PoB is not an active point or a check-in. It is a proof mechanism for the real business closed loop: who introduced what resources, which link was advanced, what results were produced, and whether the evidence has been reviewed. Only a verified closed loop can enter the value layer.</Card>
  <Card title="Level 4: Settlement and Value" icon="scale">The results of PoB verification enter the settlement layer to form traceable value attribution and distribution. This solves one of the oldest problems in Web3 - "Who should get how much" no longer relies on negotiation and relationships, but on system recording.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## minimum closed loop path

The four-tier architecture works like this in a specific Deal:

<Callout type="info">
Nodes introduce projects/resources → Deals are entered in a structured manner → Tasks are assigned to people and agents → Execution process output logs and results → Evidence is submitted to Proof Desk → PoB records are formed after approval → Enter the settlement layer for attribution and distribution
</Callout>

The key to this path is that the smallest verifiable unit of WCN is not a post, a group chat, or a superficial activity indicator, but a verified business closed loop.

## Why unified workflow is key

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Solving attribution issues" icon="search">After the traditional Web3 Deal ends, who recommends, who promotes, and who delivers all depends on verbal assertions. WCN automatically records the contribution chain of each role in the process.</Card>
  <Card title="Unlocking the true value of AI" icon="bot">Agent is no longer a standalone SaaS tool. It performs tasks within a unified system, and the output directly enters the Proof chain, and is attributed after being reviewed by humans. This is how AI truly enters the business closed loop.</Card>
  <Card title="Eliminate information asymmetry" icon="radio">All participants see the same set of data, the same progress, and the same evidence. No more gray areas like "I thought you did it" or "He said he recommended it."</Card>
  <Card title="Create a compounding network effect" icon="trending-up">Every verified closed loop is recorded as a system record. The longer the time, the clearer the node reputation, the more accurate the matching, and the harder it is for the system to be replaced - this is something that single-point tools cannot achieve.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="tip">
WCN is not a new platform, but a new business organization method - it allows Web3's cross-border, multi-party, and multi-link collaboration to have a complete infrastructure for the first time, from resource entry to value settlement, end-to-end within the same system.
</Callout>
