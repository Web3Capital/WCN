## Who is suitable to apply for a node?

For entities that can continuously introduce **verifiable business resources** to the network: projects and assets, allocated capital, deliverable services, regional or industry organizations, and authorized Agent operators. The assessment focuses on fit, repeatable delivery, and compliance boundaries, not exposure or community size.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Project/Asset side" icon="rocket">Have clear financing, growth, listing or structuring needs; be able to describe stage, data room and collaboration expectations in the form.</Card>
  <Card title="VC / Family Office / Allocate Capital" icon="coins">Provide Mandate, check size and decision-making rhythm; explain the source of deal flow and willingness to jointly invest.</Card>
  <Card title="service provider" icon="wrench">Legal affairs, security, auditing, branding, development, market making, etc. can be delivered on a contract basis; cases and service boundaries are attached.</Card>
  <Card title="Region/Industry Node" icon="globe">Able to organize projects and capital in specific jurisdictions or tracks; explain local networks and compliance constraints.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Application steps (recommended to be completed in order)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm path">Read this chapter and [10.3 How to Join](/wiki/node-onboarding/10-3-How to Join) to confirm that the goal is "node seats" and not just one-time cooperation (see 14.2 for the latter).</Step>
  <Step title="Prepare materials">The full name and official website of the organization, contact person (at least one of Email/Telegram/WeChat), position, type of node to be applied for (can be aligned with the form placeholder example: Capital/Project/Media/Institutional/Talent, etc.), **Available resources** and **Hopeful collaboration** each have a clear description; LinkedIn and supplementary instructions are optional.</Step>
  <Step title="Submit official form">Fill out and submit at [wcn.network/apply](https://wcn.network/apply); the more structured the information, the faster pre-qualification will be.</Step>
  <Step title="Prequalification">The team checks authenticity and character fit; if approved, a brief alignment meeting (video or phone) will be agreed upon.</Step>
  <Step title="Seats and Authorization">After the meeting, confirm the seat level, system permissions and responsibility boundaries; enter into signing and onboarding if necessary.</Step>
  <Step title="Online collaboration">After completing the authorization, enter the node collaboration space and participate in the Deal, Task and Proof processes according to the rules.</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning">Failure to pass the pre-review does not mean permanent rejection; if the materials are insufficient, the direction of supplementary materials will usually be explained. Please avoid providing a catchphrase introduction without a verifiable resource description.</Callout>

## What happens after submission

| Stages | Typical content | What you can expect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| T+0～2 days | Form delivery and queue sorting | Keep contact information open |
| Pre-qualification | Authenticity, conflict and matching | Possible email/IM supplementary questions |
| Alignment Meeting | Mandate, Delivery Boundaries, Compliance | Preparing for a 30-minute agency-level conversation |
| Seat confirmation | Hierarchy, authority, responsibility clauses | Internal decision-making and legal affairs (if necessary) |
| Go online | Account, space and specifications | Press onboarding to complete the first order collaboration path |

The complete cycle varies greatly depending on the institutional process and seat type; **The goal of the first round of contact is to let you know within about 48 hours** whether you have entered the in-depth conversation queue.

## Why auditing is necessary rather than open registration

<Statement>Nodes directly affect the credibility of Deal, PoB and settlement layer; the addition of each seat should improve the average quality of the network. Audit is the double threshold of governance and business quality, as well as equal respect for institutional participants.</Statement>
