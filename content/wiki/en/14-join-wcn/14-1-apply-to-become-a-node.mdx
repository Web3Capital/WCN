## The six-tier node structure

The network admits nodes across six tiers. The first four are partner tiers: an NFT binds rights and responsibility into one contract. The last two are participation tiers: they provide service or basic access without a governance seat.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Founding Partner — partner tier" icon="crown">100 seats. USD 500,000. A permanent global governance seat; the tier is never re-issued.</Card>
  <Card title="Country Partner — partner tier" icon="flag">50–65 seats. USD 50,000–500,000. One partner per country, with exclusive coordination and a 5–10% share of that country's protocol-layer revenue.</Card>
  <Card title="City Partner — partner tier" icon="building-2">150–300 seats. USD 20,000–100,000. One partner per city, anchored in local deal flow.</Card>
  <Card title="Track Partner — partner tier" icon="route">20–25 seats. USD 50,000–300,000. One partner per track, representing it across geographies.</Card>
  <Card title="Service Provider — participation tier" icon="wrench">200–500 seats. USD 30,000. Service nodes across twelve professional capabilities.</Card>
  <Card title="Standard Node — participation tier" icon="user-round">Open application. USD 5,000. Basic participation on ERC-721 plus ERC-6551, so each node carries its own account and delivery record.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="info">
A partner-tier NFT binds rights — governance, revenue share, deal-flow priority — to responsibility — bringing resources, advancing business, submitting evidence, and complying. Rights and duty sit on the same contract.
</Callout>

## Who fits a node seat

A node suits an organization that can bring **verifiable business resources** to the network over time: projects and assets, allocated capital, deliverable services, regional or track organizations, and authorized Agent operators. Review weighs fit, repeatable delivery, and compliance boundaries — not audience or community size.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Project / Asset side" icon="rocket">Holds a clear financing, growth, listing, or structuring need. Describe stage, data room, and collaboration expectations in the form.</Card>
  <Card title="Capital — VC / Fund / Family Office" icon="coins">States mandate, check size, and decision rhythm. Explain the source of deal flow and willingness to co-invest.</Card>
  <Card title="Service provider" icon="hard-hat">Delivers legal, security, audit, brand, development, or market-making work on contract. Attach cases and service boundaries.</Card>
  <Card title="Regional / Track node" icon="globe">Organizes projects and capital in a jurisdiction or track. State local reach and compliance constraints.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Application steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Confirm the path">Confirm your goal is a node seat, not a one-time pilot. For the pilot path, see 14.2.</Step>
  <Step title="Prepare materials">Gather the organization's full name and site, a contact (email, Telegram, or WeChat), your role, the node type you seek, the resources you bring, and what you seek. LinkedIn and supporting notes are optional.</Step>
  <Step title="Submit the official form">File at [wcn.network/apply](https://wcn.network/apply). The more structured your information, the faster pre-qualification runs.</Step>
  <Step title="Pre-qualification">The team checks authenticity and fit. On a pass, it proposes a short alignment call by video or phone.</Step>
  <Step title="Seat and authorization">After the call, confirm the tier, system permissions, and responsibility clauses, then proceed to signing and onboarding.</Step>
  <Step title="Onboard to collaboration">Once authorized, enter the node workspace and join the Deal, Task, and Proof of Business (PoB) processes under the rules.</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning">A failed pre-qualification is not a permanent rejection. When materials fall short, the team usually states what to add. Submit a verifiable resource description, not a headline introduction.</Callout>

## What happens after you submit

| Stage | Typical content | What to expect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| T+0–2 days | Form intake and queue sorting | Keep your contact open |
| Pre-qualification | Authenticity, conflict, and fit checks | Possible follow-up questions by email or IM |
| Alignment call | Mandate, delivery boundaries, compliance | Prepare for a 30-minute institutional conversation |
| Seat confirmation | Tier, permissions, responsibility clauses | Internal decision and legal review, where needed |
| Onboarding | Account, workspace, and rules | Begin the first collaboration path |

The full cycle varies with the institutional process and the tier. The first round aims to tell you within about 48 hours whether you have entered the in-depth queue.

## Why review, not open registration

<Statement>Nodes shape the credibility of the Deal, PoB, and settlement layers. Each added seat should raise the network's average quality. Review is the threshold for governance and business quality, and equal respect for every institutional participant.</Statement>
