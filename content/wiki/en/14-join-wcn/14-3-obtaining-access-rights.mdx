## Four-tier access model

Different participants should see different depths: **Public layer** builds consensus; **Post-application layer** supports due diligence and docking; **Node layer** carries daily collaboration; **Deal/Proof layer** is only open to relevant parties and designated review roles.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="public access layer" icon="globe">Official website, Wiki and external materials; readable by any visitor to understand the model and access rules.</Card>
  <Card title="Post-application access layer" icon="clipboard-list">Explanatory materials, process documents, or restricted catalogs available after initial intake (subject to then-current policy).</Card>
  <Card title="Node access layer" icon="id-card">Collaboration spaces, node directories and task interfaces that formal seat holders can enter; permissions are subdivided by seat type.</Card>
  <Card title="Deal / Proof layer" icon="lock">Specific deal rooms, evidence packages and attribution details; only accessible to Deal participants, service providers and authorized review roles.</Card>
</CardGrid>

---

## How to obtain permissions at each level

<Steps>
  <Step title="public layer">No account is required; directly access [wcn.network](https://wcn.network) and this Wiki.</Step>
  <Step title="Apply for back layer">After completing [Apply](https://wcn.network/apply) and passing the pre-qualification, the team will activate or send the next step materials according to type.</Step>
  <Step title="node layer">After completing the seat confirmation and authorization process described in 14.1, onboarding will activate the system identity and default scope.</Step>
  <Step title="Deal/Proof layer">When you are invited to a specific Deal or assume a Proof Desk/service delivery role, you are authorized individually according to the Deal policy; the permissions are reduced after exiting or closing the deal.</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning">There is no "one account view of the entire network data"; if certain information is not visible, it usually means that the current relationship depth or compliance requirements are not met. Forcing the boundary will destroy the trustworthy foundation of PoB and settlement.</Callout>

---

## Why clear boundaries are good for institutional actors

<Statement>Professional capital and service providers also rely on confidentiality and accountability; hierarchical permissions reduce the risk of misuse and leakage, and make reputation and attribution within the network calculable. Chaos and openness attract short-term traffic, while clear boundaries support long-term business density.</Statement>
