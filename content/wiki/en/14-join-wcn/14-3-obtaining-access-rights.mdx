## A four-layer access model

Different participants see different depths. The **public layer** builds consensus. The **post-application layer** supports due diligence and onboarding. The **node layer** carries daily collaboration. The **Deal/Proof layer** opens only to the parties and the assigned review roles.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Public layer" icon="globe">The site, this wiki, and external materials. Any visitor can read them to understand the model and the access rules.</Card>
  <Card title="Post-application layer" icon="clipboard-list">Explanatory materials, process documents, and restricted catalogs available after intake, subject to current policy.</Card>
  <Card title="Node layer" icon="id-card">Workspaces, node directories, and task interfaces for seat holders. Permissions divide by tier.</Card>
  <Card title="Deal / Proof layer" icon="lock">Specific deal rooms, evidence packages, and attribution detail. Open only to Deal parties, service providers, and authorized review roles.</Card>
</CardGrid>

---

## How each layer unlocks

<Steps>
  <Step title="Public layer">No account is needed. Visit [wcn.network](https://wcn.network) and this wiki directly.</Step>
  <Step title="Post-application layer">After you file at [Apply](https://wcn.network/apply) and pass pre-qualification, the team opens or sends the next materials by type.</Step>
  <Step title="Node layer">After the seat confirmation and authorization in 14.1, onboarding activates your system identity and default scope.</Step>
  <Step title="Deal / Proof layer">When you are invited into a specific Deal or take a Proof or service-delivery role, access is granted per Deal policy. It narrows again after you exit or the deal closes.</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning">No single account views the entire network. If something is not visible, the current relationship depth or compliance requirement is not met. Forcing the boundary would break the foundation of Proof of Business (PoB) and settlement.</Callout>

---

## Why clear boundaries serve institutions

<Statement>Capital and service providers depend on confidentiality and accountability. Layered access lowers the risk of misuse and leakage, and keeps reputation and attribution inside the network calculable. Open chaos attracts short-term traffic; clear boundaries support long-term business density.</Statement>
