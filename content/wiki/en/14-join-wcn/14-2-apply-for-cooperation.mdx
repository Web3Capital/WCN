## Who fits the cooperation path first

This path suits single-deal or framework collaborations, organizations that must clear compliance and onboarding first, and those that need several rounds of internal investment-committee approval. It fits leading projects, VCs and family offices, exchanges and liquidity partners, service providers, media and distribution networks, and regional partners tied to governments or industrial parks.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Project side" icon="rocket">Financing round, use of funds, timeline, data room, and advisory structure. State the capital or services you expect from the WCN side.</Card>

## Cooperation application steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Name the cooperation type">Pick the main tag from the four categories above. Prepare one or two workable hypotheses, such as connecting audit partners to Southeast Asian seed projects.</Step>
  <Step title="Submit structured information">File at [wcn.network/apply](https://wcn.network/apply). Put delivery capability in **Resources you can provide**, your need in **Looking for**, and mark "Partnership" or the specific form in **Node type / Role**.</Step>
  <Step title="Counterpart sorting">Your application enters the matching business line. A reply on whether to proceed usually arrives within about 48 hours.</Step>
  <Step title="Agreement and pilot">For large or sensitive scope, the parties may sign an NDA or framework agreement first, then verify through a pilot deal or task.</Step>
  <Step title="Optional: node conversion">Once the relationship holds and two-way delivery has been verified, you can start the node-seat process in 14.1 to unify permissions and settlement.</Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info">If an existing node referred you, name the referrer and the background in **Why WCN** or the contact field. This supports priority sorting and conflict checks.</Callout>

## Cooperation versus node

Cooperation records a **business relationship and a delivery agreement**. A node additionally binds a **seat in the system, default permissions, and long-term responsibility**. Many institutions run one or two closed loops first, then weigh whether to take on node duties.

<Statement>WCN is both a node network and an institutional collaboration layer. The cooperation entrance lets a qualified counterpart open a dialogue without first settling whether to become a node.</Statement>
