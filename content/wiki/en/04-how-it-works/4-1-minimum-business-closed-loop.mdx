# The smallest unit of WCN is not content, relationship or activity, but a verifiable business closed loop.
</Hero>

<MetaGrid>
  <Meta label="core definition" value="Resources enter + execution advances + results are verified = closed loop" />
  <Meta label="key significance" value="Closed loop is the only criterion for the system to distinguish between noise and value" />
  <Meta label="TradFi Benchmarking" value="Deal Completion Rate of PE/VC" />
</MetaGrid>

<Statement>Minimum business closed loop = resources are introduced + tasks are executed + results are verified. If one of the three is missing, it is not considered a closed loop.</Statement>

---

## Three necessary conditions for closed loop

<Steps>
  <Step title="There are resources to enter">Nodes bring real projects, capital, services or regional resources into the network. Resources must be specific—a project being financed, an allocation of funds, a deliverable service—not “know some people.”</Step>
  <Step title="There is implementation progress">People and Agents advance resources from "opportunities" to "results" in Deal/Task. Advancing means that someone is responsible, that actions take place, and that status changes - rather than "having a group chat."</Step>
  <Step title="There are results verified">Evidence is submitted, reviewed and approved, and attribution is clear. The results go into PoB records. Verification means having third-party verifiable evidence—not “he said it was done.”</Step>
</Steps>

---

## What counts as a closed loop and what doesn’t?

This is the fundamental difference between WCN and all “activity reward” systems:

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="✅ Calculate closed loop" icon="circle-check">The seed round financing is completed (with agreement + payment record); the forensic audit delivery is accepted (with SOW + deliverables); the market making service is online (with contract + on-chain liquidity data); the project is recommended and successfully listed (with communication record + online evidence).</Card>
  <Card title="⊘ Not considered a closed loop" icon="ban">Two people were introduced but there was no follow-up; the Deck was shared but no progress was made; a meeting was held but there were no action items; the Agent did research but no one adopted it; a tweet was sent but no business was converted.</Card>
</CardGrid>

> **Industry Analogy**: In TradFi PE/VC, "Deal Completion Rate" is the core indicator - it is not about how many projects you are exposed to, but how many projects you have actually closed. A VC might look at 3,000 projects a year, but Deal Completion only sees 10-15. The closed-loop concept of WCN is equivalent to this: only the closed Deal enters the value layer.

<Callout type="warning">
The judgment standard is very simple: if the action is removed and the business results still occur, then the action does not form part of the closed loop. Only participation that contributes causally to the outcome counts.
</Callout>

---

## Standard closed loop path

<Callout type="info">
Node introduces resources → Opportunities enter Deal/Task → People and Agents execute and advance → Result formation → Evidence enters Proof Desk → Review and pass → Generate PoB record → Enter settlement layer
</Callout>

Key Features: Each step on the path has a clear **owner** (who is responsible), **status** (current status) and **output** (what is produced). There are no "ownerless tasks" and "stateless progress".

---

## Why does Web3 not have this concept so far?

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="There is no unified definition of results" icon="ruler">The definition of "success" in Web3 is highly fragmented - some people look at TVL, some look at the number of people in the community, and some look at the token price. No system uses "real business completion" as a unified measurement.</Card>
  <Card title="No evidence infrastructure" icon="paperclip">TradFi has a complete audit chain (Big Four, law firms, custodian banks). In the multi-party collaboration of Web3, evidence is scattered on Telegram, Email, Google Docs and the blockchain, and there is no unified collection and review system.</Card>
  <Card title="Incentive design rewards process rather than results" icon="gift">Airdrops, points, check-ins, social tasks—these mechanisms reward "actions done" rather than "results produced." The system is therefore flooded with noise.</Card>
  <Card title="Attribution difficulties lead to avoidance" icon="circle-help">In multi-party collaboration, it is extremely difficult to determine who contributed how much to the outcome. Most projects choose to avoid this issue (split equally or rely on relationship negotiation) rather than establish a systematic attribution mechanism.</Card>
</CardGrid>

---

## Why is the closed loop the origin of the system?

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Define value boundaries" icon="ruler">Closing the loop is the only criterion for WCN to distinguish between "worth recording" and "not worth recording". No closed loop → No PoB → No settlement entrance → No value distribution.</Card>
  <Card title="Drive Incentive Design" icon="lightbulb">All incentives and settlements revolve around closed loops. This means that the system's resource allocation naturally points to "people who can promote closed loops" rather than "people who can make noise."</Card>
  <Card title="Anchoring execution quality" icon="target">A closed loop requires that each step has an owner, a state, and an output. This forces participants to perform rather than pretend to be busy.</Card>
  <Card title="Accumulate network value" icon="gem">Each verified closed loop is precipitated into system assets: node reputation, matching accuracy, attribution data, and agent training materials. The longer time passes, the harder it is for the system to be replaced.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="tip">
All subsequent structures of WCN - PoB, settlement, reputation, governance - are based on the minimum business closed loop. By understanding the closed loop, you understand why WCN can extract real value from noise.
</Callout>
