# The smallest unit of WCN is not content, a relationship, or activity. It is a verifiable business loop.
</Hero>

<MetaGrid>
  <Meta label="Core definition" value="Resource enters + work advances + result is verified = one loop" />
  <Meta label="Why it matters" value="The loop is WCN's only test for separating noise from value" />
  <Meta label="TradFi reference" value="Deal completion rate in PE and VC" />
</MetaGrid>

<Statement>A minimum business loop equals a resource introduced, plus a task executed, plus a result verified. Remove any one of the three and it is not a loop.</Statement>

---

## Three necessary conditions

<Steps>
  <Step title="A resource enters">A node brings a real project, capital, service, or regional resource into the network. The resource must be specific — a round being raised, an allocation to deploy, a service to deliver — not "I know some people."</Step>
  <Step title="Work advances">People and agents move the resource from an opportunity to a result inside a Deal and its Tasks. Advancing means someone owns it, actions occur, and status changes — not a group chat.</Step>
  <Step title="A result is verified">Evidence is submitted, reviewed, and approved, and attribution is clear. The result enters the Proof ledger. Verification means third-party-verifiable evidence — not "he said it was done."</Step>
</Steps>

---

## What counts, and what does not

This is the line between WCN and every activity-reward system.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Counts as a loop" icon="circle-check">A seed round closes, with agreement and payment record. A forensic audit is accepted, with SOW and deliverables. A market-making service goes live, with contract and onchain liquidity data. A project is introduced and lists, with communication record and launch evidence.</Card>
  <Card title="Does not count" icon="ban">Two parties are introduced but nothing follows. A deck is shared but no work advances. A meeting happens but produces no action items. An agent runs research that no one adopts. A post goes out but converts no business.</Card>
</CardGrid>

> **Industry reference.** In PE and VC, deal completion rate is a core metric. It does not count how many projects you see; it counts how many you actually close. A fund may screen 3,000 projects a year and complete 10 to 15. WCN's loop is the same test: only a closed deal enters the value layer.

<Callout type="warning">
The test is simple. Remove an action, and if the business result still occurs, that action is not part of the loop. Only participation that causally contributes to the outcome counts.
</Callout>

---

## The standard loop path

<Callout type="info">
A node introduces a resource → the opportunity enters a Deal and its Tasks → people and agents execute and advance → a result forms → evidence enters the Proof Desk → review passes → a Verification Node is generated → the entry proceeds to Settlement.
</Callout>

Each step on the path carries a clear **owner**, a status, and an output. There are no ownerless tasks and no stateless progress.

---

## Why a result is verified: four conditions

A loop only verifies when all four conditions hold. They come straight from how Proof of Business reconciles a result.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="The result is externally observable" icon="eye">A signing, a wire, a launch, a delivery, or a KPI met — visible outside the parties' own account.</Card>
  <Card title="The evidence is verifiable" icon="paperclip">Contracts, receipts, onchain records, counterparty confirmation, and reviewable logs. Words alone do not verify.</Card>
  <Card title="Responsibility is traceable" icon="route">Each advancing action maps to a specific node or agent. No anonymous contribution enters the record.</Card>
  <Card title="The process is re-reviewable" icon="search">A reviewer re-examining the process reaches the same judgment. Verification must reproduce, not depend on one person's read.</Card>
</CardGrid>

---

## Why Web3 has lacked this concept

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="No shared definition of result" icon="ruler">Definitions of success are fragmented — TVL for some, member counts for others, token price for the rest. No system uses real business completion as the common measure.</Card>
  <Card title="No evidence infrastructure" icon="paperclip">TradFi has a full audit chain: large auditors, law firms, custodians. In Web3, evidence scatters across chat, email, documents, and the chain, with no shared collection and review.</Card>
  <Card title="Incentives reward process, not result" icon="gift">Airdrops, points, check-ins, and social tasks reward actions taken, not results produced. The system then fills with noise.</Card>
  <Card title="Attribution is hard, so teams avoid it" icon="circle-help">In multi-party work, fixing who contributed how much is difficult. Most projects split evenly or negotiate by relationship rather than build a systematic attribution mechanism.</Card>
</CardGrid>

---

## Why the loop is the origin of the system

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="It defines the value boundary" icon="ruler">The loop is WCN's only test for what is worth recording. No loop means no PoB, no entry to Settlement, and no value distribution.</Card>
  <Card title="It drives incentive design" icon="lightbulb">Every incentive and settlement turns on the loop. Resource allocation then points to operators who advance loops, not operators who make noise.</Card>
  <Card title="It anchors execution quality" icon="target">A loop requires an owner, a status, and an output at each step. That requirement forces real work instead of the appearance of work.</Card>
  <Card title="It accumulates network value" icon="gem">Each verified loop settles into a system asset: node reputation, matching accuracy, attribution data, and agent training material. The record grows harder to replace over time.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="tip">
Every later structure in WCN — PoB, settlement, reputation, governance — rests on the minimum business loop. Understand the loop, and you understand how WCN extracts real value from noise.
</Callout>
