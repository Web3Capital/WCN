## The setup

**The project.** An AI and DeFi infrastructure project — team in Singapore, technology in Europe — raising a $2M seed round. It needs two things.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="The right capital" icon="coins">Funds that understand the AI and DeFi intersection, accept a SAFE plus Token Warrant structure, and write tickets of $200K to $500K.</Card>
  <Card title="A complete legal structure" icon="file-stack">SAFE agreement review, Token Warrant terms, Singapore entity compliance check, and an investor KYC process.</Card>
</CardGrid>

**The parties in the WCN network.**

<CardGrid cols={3}>
  <Card title="Capital node A" icon="landmark">Hong Kong fund, AI and Infra track, seed ticket of $300K to $500K.</Card>
  <Card title="Capital node B" icon="landmark">Dubai family office, DeFi infrastructure, ticket of $500K to $1M.</Card>
  <Card title="Service node C" icon="scale">Singapore legal team, specialized in SAFE, SAFT, and Token Warrant structures.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Research Agent" icon="microscope">Structures project data, runs competitive analysis, marks risk, and drafts the investment memo.</Card>
  <Card title="Execution Agent" icon="zap">Produces meeting minutes, tracks to-dos, sends reminders, and monitors document-collection status.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## The fourteen-day loop

### Day 1–2: resource entry and matching

<CardGrid cols={1}>
  <Card title="Day 1: project entry" icon="inbox">The project node submits to Project Intake: deck, tokenomics draft, team background, technical documents, and financing terms ($2M SAFE plus Token Warrant). The Research Agent returns a structured summary, a risk list, and a competitive comparison within two hours.</Card>
  <Card title="Day 2: matching and Deal Room" icon="handshake">The Deal Agent matches nodes A and B on their stated preferences. The system opens a Deal Room, names the Deal owner — the regional node that sourced the project — and adds service node C to the legal track. All parties see the same structured information.</Card>
</CardGrid>

### Day 3–7: execution advances

<CardGrid cols={1}>
  <Card title="Day 3: first meeting" icon="phone">The Deal owner convenes a call between the project and capital node A. The Execution Agent produces minutes in real time and extracts five action items with owners. All participants receive structured minutes within 30 minutes.</Card>
  <Card title="Day 4–5: diligence and legal in parallel" icon="zap">Capital node A requests a supplementary technical audit and founder background check; the Research Agent assembles both within two hours. In parallel, service node C reviews the SAFE plus Token Warrant terms and issues a preliminary legal opinion on Day 5.</Card>
  <Card title="Day 6: second round and node B enters" icon="phone">Capital node A confirms intent to follow at $400K. Capital node B requests a separate meeting after reviewing the materials. The Deal Room runs two capital lines at once, each tracked separately.</Card>
  <Card title="Day 7: terms" icon="clipboard-list">The project and capital node A agree on valuation and the Token Warrant ratio. Service node C issues the final legal opinion. The Execution Agent tracks the status of every document awaiting signature.</Card>
</CardGrid>

### Day 8–11: signing and payment

<CardGrid cols={1}>
  <Card title="Day 8–9: agreements signed" icon="pen-line">Capital node A signs a SAFE ($400K) plus Token Warrant. Capital node B signs a SAFE ($500K) plus Token Warrant. Service node C completes the compliance review of all agreements.</Card>
  <Card title="Day 10–11: payment confirmed" icon="banknote">Capital node A wires $400K. Capital node B sends $500K ($300K by wire, $200K in USDC). The Execution Agent tracks payment status and links the bank confirmations to the onchain records.</Card>
</CardGrid>

### Day 12–14: evidence and Proof

<CardGrid cols={1}>
  <Card title="Day 12: evidence packaged" icon="paperclip">The Deal owner submits to the Proof Desk: signed SAFE agreements (2), Token Warrants (2), bank payment confirmations (2), the onchain USDC transfer hash (1), the legal opinion (1), and all meeting minutes (4).</Card>
  <Card title="Day 13: review" icon="search">An independent reviewer checks completeness and authenticity, and confirms: the financing total is $900K for this tranche, the agreement terms match the intake, the legal delivery is complete, and the timeline is consistent.</Card>
  <Card title="Day 14: Verification Node and attribution" icon="circle-check">Review passes. The system reconciles the result across the three ledgers, generates a Verification Node in the Proof ledger, and assigns attribution as below.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Service node C" icon="scale">Share: 18%. Issued the legal opinion, reviewed the agreements, and confirmed compliance.</Card>
  <Card title="Agent contribution" icon="bot">Share: 12%. The Research Agent's summary was adopted by a capital node as a decision input, proven by logs; the Execution Agent's minutes and reminders advanced process efficiency.</Card>

<Callout type="info">
Attribution shares are not a fixed template. The system calculates them from the evidence chain. If a Research Agent report is not adopted, its share is zero — only output that is adopted and enters the loop carries value.
</Callout>

## The same work without WCN

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Without WCN" icon="ban">The project sends a deck to 20 people at a conference. Five reply. Three schedule meetings, which take 2 to 3 weeks to coordinate. Feedback follows the meetings over 1 to 2 weeks. Terms move through email and Telegram over 1 to 2 weeks. Finding a law firm takes 1 to 2 weeks. Signing and payment take a week. No system records who sourced whom or who advanced what. Typical cycle: 6 to 10 weeks, with the record scattered across Telegram, email, and documents — and no path to settlement.</Card>
  <Card title="With WCN" icon="circle-check">Project entry is structured. An agent completes first-pass screening and matching within two hours. The Deal Room keeps every party in sync. Legal and financing advance in parallel. The Execution Agent removes reminder and sync time. Evidence is collected and attribution is calculated. Typical cycle: 14 days, with the full evidence chain intact, attribution defined, and settlement ready.</Card>
</CardGrid>

> **Where the gain comes from.** WCN does not make investors decide faster; the time to form an investment judgment does not change. It removes coordination friction — information sync, follow-up chasing, document assembly, and legal coordination. That coordination tax runs 30 to 40 percent of the time in the traditional process. Structured workflow and agent support compress it toward zero.

## What the system gained

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="The project" icon="target">Closed a $900K tranche plus the legal structure in 14 days. Every collaboration record is intact and reusable as trust evidence for later rounds.</Card>
  <Card title="The capital side" icon="chart-bar">Structured data lowers diligence cost. The investment record enters the system, building investor standing and match accuracy.</Card>
  <Card title="The service provider" icon="clipboard-list">The legal deliverable is documented and attributed. It is no longer an informal favor; it is a formal contribution with a clear basis for settlement.</Card>
  <Card title="The network" icon="gem">One complete loop enters the system. Participant reputation updates, and the matching model gains new training data. Similar deals run more efficiently next time.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="tip">
This is the difference between WCN and an intro group, a CRM, or a points tool. A collaboration not only produces a result; it becomes a system asset — each loop makes the network harder to replace.
</Callout>
