## Human nodes: four areas that stay with people

In WCN, human nodes are not targets for replacement. They are the strategic control layer. Under current conditions, four areas **must stay under human responsibility**.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Resource introduction and relationships" icon="globe">Bringing projects, capital, services, and regional resources into the network. High-value B2B relationships rest on trust, judgment, and reputation between people. An agent can assist research; it cannot replace who you know and who trusts you.</Card>
  <Card title="Strategic judgment and priority" icon="compass">Deciding what advances, what pauses, and what moves to the next stage. Current models are strong at aggregation and pattern recognition, but business judgment, market timing, and political sensitivity still need human experience.</Card>
  <Card title="Trust and the responsibility it carries" icon="handshake">When a multi-million-dollar decision needs a signature, the counterparty weighs the recommender's reputation and legal liability, not a model's score. High-value Web3 deals remain a trust exchange between people.</Card>
  <Card title="Final accountability and compliance" icon="scale">Signing contracts, sending payments, issuing legal opinions, reaching tax conclusions, and filing with regulators carry a defined legal subject. When an overstep or error occurs, responsibility falls on the node, not the model.</Card>
</CardGrid>

> **Industry reference.** In 2024 Goldman Sachs deployed internal AI to assist IPO due diligence and contract review, yet MD-level bankers still sign every final decision. JPMorgan's contract review system processes a high volume of documents, but a lawyer confirms each legal conclusion. That is the current boundary of practice.

## Agents: where they apply best

The value of an agent is not full automation. It is amplifying a human node's output within defined boundaries.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Research Agent" icon="microscope">Project summary, competitive analysis, risk marking, and market data assembly. It compresses a multi-day analyst task into minutes. It outputs a structured report that a human reviews, then adopts or revises.</Card>
  <Card title="Deal Agent" icon="clipboard-list">Project-to-capital match suggestions, first-pass screening scores, a due-diligence checklist, and deal progress tracking. It does not make investment decisions; it prepares the inputs a decision needs.</Card>
  <Card title="Growth Agent" icon="trending-up">Content drafting — project summaries, evidence-based copy, social posts — plus channel matching and attribution tracking. It standardizes the distribution capability of a BD team.</Card>
  <Card title="Execution Agent" icon="zap">Meeting minutes, to-do tracking, reminders, status reports, and document-collection prompts. It removes the coordination tax that consumes 30 to 40 percent of deal time.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Statement>An agent's core role is to structure and replicate the methods of strong nodes — so the working method of one top operator can serve a hundred ordinary nodes.</Statement>

## The real boundary of AI capability

The division of work follows what AI can actually do, not a marketing narrative.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Where AI is strong" icon="circle-check">Information aggregation and structuring; pattern recognition and anomaly detection; translation and localization; process automation and status tracking; large-scale filtering and sorting.</Card>
  <Card title="Where AI is weak" icon="ban">Judgments with legal consequence; strategic choices in multi-party games; cultural and political risk; timing that needs industry intuition; trust that rests on personal reputation.</Card>
  <Card title="Where AI is improving but not yet reliable" icon="triangle-alert">Long reasoning chains, where accuracy falls past several steps; real-time fact verification, where hallucination is not fully solved; decision consistency, where the same input can yield different output.</Card>
  <Card title="WCN's design response" icon="shield">Every agent output defaults to a recommendation, not a decision. Key actions require human approval. Agent logs are kept intact for audit and dispute review.</Card>
</CardGrid>

## Control framework: what happens when an agent errs

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Output review" icon="search">Every key agent output — recommendation, report, match result — is reviewed by a human before it goes out. The reviewer can approve, revise, or reject.</Card>
  <Card title="Capability and task scope" icon="lock-keyhole">Each agent carries a Capability Manifest that fixes its boundary, and a Task Contract that authorizes scope per task. Read tasks run automatically; access and transaction actions require approval.</Card>
  <Card title="Audit trail" icon="file-text">Every input, reasoning step, and output is recorded. When a result is wrong, a reviewer can trace the exact step and the data the judgment rested on.</Card>
  <Card title="Rollback" icon="undo">If a problem surfaces after a recommendation is adopted, the system marks it corrected and updates every downstream decision that relied on it.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="warning">
WCN's design principle for agents: **a step slower beats a step wrong.** In high-value Web3 deals, one bad recommendation or one leak can cost millions. An agent's value depends on staying controllable.
</Callout>

## Boundary summary

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Stays with the person" icon="lock">Signing, payment, legal and tax conclusions, final Proof approval, high-risk compliance judgment, major resource commitment, and speaking for the network externally.</Card>
  <Card title="Can move to an agent" icon="bot">Research, screening, match suggestions, follow-up reminders, minute generation, information sorting, status tracking, first-pass attribution calculation, and monitoring.</Card>
  <Card title="Needs human approval first" icon="hand">Any external send — email or message — confirmation of key documents, transaction-structure suggestions, and actions in sensitive jurisdictions.</Card>
  <Card title="Must leave a complete log" icon="file-text">Every agent read, recommendation, contact, verification, marking, reminder, and output carries a timestamp and its context.</Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="tip">
WCN does not chase the narrative of full automation. It makes the agent a controlled, auditable, settleable execution layer — guided by human judgment, scaling the network's execution by an order of magnitude.
</Callout>
